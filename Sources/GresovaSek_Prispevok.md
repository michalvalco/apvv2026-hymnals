# Grešová-Sekelská, Adriana — Príspevok k vzniku a obsahu Cithary sanctorum (1636) Juraja Tranovského (2021)

## Citation
GREŠOVÁ-SEKELSKÁ, Adriana: Príspevok k vzniku a obsahu Cithary sanctorum (1636) Juraja Tranovského [On the origin and contents of Georgius Tranoscius' Cithara sanctorum ("Lyre of the Saints," 1636)]. In: *Clavibus unitis* 10/4 (2021), pp. 1–10. VEGA 1/0331/20. Published by Association for Central European Cultural Studies.

## Summary
A peer-reviewed article presenting results of the author's doctoral research (FiF UK 2020). The study reconstructs the first edition of the CS (1636) from four surviving defective copies (Liptovský Mikuláš, Martin, Prague, Valašské Meziříčí) plus a Budapest microfilm of a now-lost fifth copy, and analyses the repertoire's composition by confessional origin, chronological layers, genre, and the extent of Tranovský's original contributions.

The article includes four graphs quantifying: (1) textual origins of CS hymns by source, (2) chronological dating of the repertoire, (3) confessional origin of adopted hymns, and (4) confessional background of the Czech-language repertoire specifically. The study also surveys liturgical books available in Liptov parishes before the CS, based on canonical visitation records from c. 1615.

## Key Arguments

### Reconstruction of the first edition:
- All four surviving copies of the 1636 edition are significantly defective (dozens of missing pages each). The base copy (Liptovský Mikuláš microfilm) was missing 122 hymns. By cross-referencing all copies, 1 complete hymn and 16 partial hymns remain unrecoverable from 1636 copies alone — these can be supplemented from the 1653 edition, the closest subsequent edition. (p. 4)
- Haan's (1875) supposed description of the first edition title page is unreliable — his account does not correspond to the actual first edition. He appears to have been working from later editions (post-1741). "Ani Ľudovít Haan nemal v rukách exemplár z roku 1636." (p. 4)

### Liturgical context in Liptov (c. 1615):
- Canonical visitation records from Superintendent Eliáš Láni (c. 1615) show that churches in the Liptov region contained primarily Catholic liturgical books: Esztergom missals, graduals, psalters, antiphonaries. Lutheran kancionáls were present but not dominant — the Kunvaldský kancionál (1576) was the most frequent. (p. 6)
- The newer Lutheran Závorkov kancionál (1602) is absent from all Liptov churches in the visitation records. (p. 6)
- This implies that Tranovský encountered a worship environment still substantially shaped by Catholic/Esztergom liturgical tradition, which "could have influenced the form of the Cithara sanctorum hymnbook." (abstract, p. 1)

### Content composition — genres:
- The CS contains all parts of the mass ordinary. The practice of *missa brevis* is evident (Kyrie and Gloria as standard; Sanctus, Benedictus, and Agnus Dei only during services with communion). Luther's removal of the offertory is reflected: only introits survive from the proper. The largest group numerically is *ofíciové spevy* (office hymns). (p. 6)

### Melodic sources:
- At least 186 items were notated in the first edition. Nearly 30 different Bohemian hymnological sources identified as melodic antecedents. (p. 6)
- Three most important melodic sources: (1) Utraquist Kolínský kancionál (1517) — the largest single melodic source; (2) Brethren Šamotulský kancionál (1561); (3) Lutheran Kunvaldský kancionál (1576). (p. 6)
- Calvinist psalm melodies: "Tranovský používal rád" (Tranovský used them gladly). His new texts were frequently set to French psalm tunes. These melodies are "dokonca častejšie ako Tranovského vlastné melódie" (even more frequent than Tranovský's own melodies). (p. 6)
- 12 melodies tentatively attributed to Tranovský (revised down from the 8 in the 2023 publication — or rather, the count evolved as research continued). (p. 6)
