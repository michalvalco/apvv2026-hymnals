# Chalupský, Michal — Obřad křtu podle Agendy české z r. 1581 (2024)

## Citation
CHALUPSKI, Michal: Obřad křtu podle Agendy české z r. 1581 [Baptismal Ceremony according to Agenda česká of 1581]. In: *Teologická reflexe* 30 (2024/1), pp. 43–54. DOI: 10.14712/27880796.2024.3. CC BY 4.0.

## Summary
A peer-reviewed article analysing the baptismal rite of the Agenda Česká (1581) by comparing it with its German source — the Wittenberg *Agenda das ist Kirchenordnung* (1539/1540) — and with Luther's *Taufbüchlein* (1523, 1526). The article also surveys baptismal practice across the Bohemian confessional spectrum: Catholic (*Agenda Pragensis*, c. 1476–79), conservative Utraquist (Křižovnická agenda, 1581), Kutná Hora agenda (Václav Štefan Teplický), Závorka Lipenský's *Pravidlo* (1607), and the Unity of Brethren baptismal agenda (1580/1612). Published at ETF UK Prague.

## Key Arguments
- The Agenda Česká's baptismal rite is based on the Wittenberg church order of 1539/1540, itself built on Luther's Taufbüchlein of 1526. The editors' approach was "to some extent creative" — not a mechanical translation. (abstract, p. 43)
- Key modifications from the German source: exorcism made optional (not obligatory as in the Wittenberg order); baptism by threefold pouring rather than immersion; no mention of handing over baptismal robes; different ordering of elements (Gospel read before the flood prayer, not after); double question on willingness to be baptised (possibly preserved from older Czech tradition). (pp. 49–53)
- The Agenda Česká "continues the simplification of baptismal liturgy begun by Martin Luther and in this respect goes beyond its source." (abstract/p. 54)
- The editors' anonymity is noted — we know only that they were "an international group of Lutheran-oriented clergy" ordained mostly at Wittenberg, who agreed in doctrine but differed in ceremonial practice. (p. 46)
- The Agenda Česká influenced the liturgy of the Unity of Brethren (citing Landová 2014, p. 64). (p. 47, fn. 15)
- The Agenda Česká includes the feast of Master Jan Hus among the minor feasts — evidence of adaptation to Czech context despite Lutheran confessional alignment. (p. 47, fn. 15)
- Chalupský notes that the Agenda Česká "still has not received detailed treatment" (p. 46) — confirming the scholarly gap our chapter addresses.

## Relation to TEPALIT Chapter
- **Which locus/section:** §4.4 (Authority of Scripture and Tradition — the simplification of baptismal rites reflects the *sola Scriptura* principle applied to liturgy); §2 (Confessional Context — the comparative survey of baptismal rites across confessions); §4.1 (indirectly — the same editorial method Chalupský documents for baptism likely applies to the Eucharistic rite)
- **How it supports our argument:** Demonstrates that the Agenda Česká editors exercised creative editorial freedom with their German Lutheran source — they were not slavish translators. This confirms the pattern visible in the Eucharistic rite: the editors mediated between German Lutheran models and Czech/Bohemian pastoral reality. The optional exorcism and the preservation of the double baptismal question from older Czech tradition show the same kind of "irenic register" we identified in the Eucharistic analysis — Lutheran in substance, accommodating in form.
- **Methodological value:** Chalupský's comparative table format (Luther 1526 → Wittenberg 1540 → Agenda Česká 1581) provides a model for how we could structure our own comparison of Eucharistic formulations.
- **Limitation:** Focuses on baptism, not the Eucharist. The article does not engage with Eucharistic theology. However, the editorial method it documents is transferable.
