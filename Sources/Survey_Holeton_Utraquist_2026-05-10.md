## §2 — NEW Essential items (10–20 target; 14 delivered)

### Anglophone Holeton corpus — BRRP and adjacent

These are individually accessible PDFs and form the core of "Utraquist position-being-received" as interpretive ground for the chapter.

#### 2.1 Holeton, David R. — "The Bohemian Eucharistic Movement in its European Context" (BRRP 1, 1996)
- *BRRP* 1 (1994/pub. 1996): 23–47.
- [Open PDF](http://brrp.org/proceedings/brrp1/holeton.pdf)
- **Signature:** Situates the lay-chalice and frequent-communion impulses of Jakoubek-era Utraquism in the European devotional landscape — the prehistory of what Slovak Lutheran usage will later receive *as* "the Czech tradition." Anchors §2 (confessional context) and §4.1 (Eucharistic locus).
- Relevance: §2, §4.1.

#### 2.2 Holeton, David R. — "The Evolution of Utraquist Eucharistic Liturgy: a textual study" (BRRP 2, 1998)
- *BRRP* 2 (1996/pub. 1998): 97–126.
- [Open PDF](https://brrp.org/proceedings/brrp2/holeton.pdf)
- **Signature:** Single most important textual map of how the Utraquist Mass evolved 1417–1620. Companion to Graham 2006 (already in index) but with broader textual base. Direct grounding for §4.1 and §5 (where the Mass texts inherited by Slovak Lutheran usage diverge from CS hymnody).
- Relevance: §4.1, §5.

#### 2.3 Holeton, David R. — "All Manner of Wonder Under the Sun: A Curious Development in the Evolution of Utraquist Eucharistic Liturgy" (BRRP 3, 2000)
- *BRRP* 3 (1998/pub. 2000): 161–172.
- [Open PDF](http://brrp.org/proceedings/brrp3/holeton.pdf)
- **Signature:** Late-Utraquist ritual oddity — useful as a control case showing how far Utraquist liturgy could drift before Lutheranisation pressure tightened it.
- Relevance: §4.1.

#### 2.4 Holeton, David R. — "The Sacramental Theology of Tomáš Štítný of Štítné" (BRRP 4, 2002)
- *BRRP* 4 (2000/pub. 2002): 57–80.
- [Open PDF](http://www.brrp.org/proceedings/brrp4/holeton.pdf)
- **Signature:** Vernacular pre-Hussite sacramentology; the deep substrate beneath Jakoubek and the chalice movement. Helps trace the *long* Utraquist Eucharistic theology that Lutheran reception both inherited and re-coded.
- Relevance: §3, §4.1.

#### 2.5 Holeton, David R. — "Fynes Moryson's Itinerary: A Sixteenth Century English Traveller's Observations on Bohemia, its Reformation, and its Liturgy" (BRRP 5/II, 2005)
- *BRRP* 5/II (2002/pub. 2005): 379–411.
- [Open PDF](https://www.brrp.org/proceedings/brrp5b/holeton.pdf)
- **Signature:** External (English Protestant) eyewitness on what Utraquist worship looked like c. 1591 — exactly the moment when Cithara Sanctorum's eventual sources were being formed. Useful for §3 historiographic framing and §4.1 mass-texture description.
- Relevance: §3, §4.1.

#### 2.6 Holeton, David R., Vlhová-Wörner, Hana, & Bílková, Milena — "*Gregorius presul* in Bohemian Liturgy and Iconography" (BRRP 6, 2007)
- *BRRP* 6 (2004/pub. 2007): 215–245.
- [Open PDF](http://www.brrp.org/proceedings/brrp6/holeton.pdf) (verify URL)
- **Signature:** A trope-level case study showing Utraquist creative continuity with Latin chant traditions; methodological model for §5 transfer analysis (single liturgical element traced across confessional boundary).
- Relevance: §4.1, §5.

#### 2.7 Holeton, David R., & Vlhová-Wörner, Hana — "A Remarkable Witness to the Feast of Saint Jan Hus" (BRRP 7, 2009)
- *BRRP* 7 (2006/pub. 2009): 156–184.
- [Open PDF](http://www.brrp.org/proceedings/brrp7/holeton.pdf) (verify URL)
- **Signature:** Hus liturgical commemoration in a recovered antiphonary. Bridges to the Stöckel-era question of how/whether Slovak Lutherans retained Hus-feast hymnody in CS — a §5 question the chapter must address.
- Relevance: §5.

#### 2.8 Holeton, David R. — "The Evolution of the Celebration of the Daily Office in Utraquism: an overview" (BRRP 8, 2011)
- *BRRP* 8 (2008/pub. 2011): 198–222. *Filosofický časopis*, Special Issue 3.
- [Open PDF](http://www.brrp.org/proceedings/brrp8/holeton.pdf)
- **Signature:** Daily Office (matins/vespers) as Utraquist site of vernacularisation and lay participation. The Office is where Utraquist→Lutheran continuity was most pronounced (the Cithara Sanctorum hours-pattern derives heavily from this stratum). **Critical for §4.1 and §5.**
- Relevance: §4.1, §5.

#### 2.9 Holeton, David R. — "The Place of Children in Utraquist Liturgical Song" (BRRP 9, 2014)
- *BRRP* 9 (2010/pub. 2014): 111–137.
- [Open PDF](http://www.brrp.org/proceedings/brrp9/holeton.pdf) (verify URL)
- **Signature:** Children's role in Utraquist hymnody. Pertinent to whether the chapter discusses domestic/catechetical reception of CS — Slovak Lutheran *Kinderlieder* came partly through Utraquist channels.
- Relevance: §4.1, §5 (catechesis-hymn axis).

#### 2.10 Holeton, David R. — "Sacramental and Liturgical Reform in Late Medieval Bohemia" (1987)
- *Studia Liturgica* 17/2 (1987): 87–96.
- Print-only (verify *Studia Liturgica* archive at sagepub).
- **Signature:** The earliest Holeton statement of his sacramental-reform thesis; pre-1989 scholarly framework. Foundational and often cited.
- Relevance: §4.1.

#### 2.11 Holeton, David R. — "The Communion of Infants and Hussitism" (1984)
- *Communio Viatorum* 27/4 (1984): 207–225.
- Print-only / Charles University ETF archive.
- Companion: **"The Communion of Infants: The Basel Years"**, *Communio Viatorum* 29/2 (1986): 15–40.
- **Signature:** Holeton's two articles on the *unique* Utraquist practice of paedo-communion (lasting from 1417 to ~1500). Important *contrast point* — what Slovak Lutheran reception did NOT inherit. Useful for showing the selective filter operating in transfer.
- Relevance: §4.1 (negative/excluded element); methodologically valuable.

#### 2.12 Holeton, David R. — *La communion des tout-petits enfants: étude du mouvement eucharistique en Bohême vers le fin du Moyen-âge*. Bibliotheca Ephemerides Liturgicae, Subsidia 50. Roma: Edizioni Liturgiche, 1989. 286 pp.
- French monograph; thesis-derived definitive treatment of Utraquist paedo-communion.
- **Signature:** Definitive monograph behind the two *Communio Viatorum* articles. Cite when making any claim about Utraquist paedo-communion as a sociological-theological practice. ILL likely needed.
- Relevance: §4.1.

### Anglophone bridging volumes — Hussite/Utraquist–Reformation reception

#### 2.13 Van Dussen, Michael, & Soukup, Pavel (eds.) — *A Companion to the Hussites*. Brill's Companions to the Christian Tradition 90. Leiden/Boston: Brill, 2020. xii+454 pp. ISBN 9789004397866. €199 / $239.

  - Mutlová, Petra — "Major Hussite Theologians before the Compactata"
  - Cermanová, Pavlína — "The Apocalyptic Background of Hussite Radicalism"
  - Zilynská, Blanka — "The Utraquist Church after the Compactata"
  - Coufal, Dušan — "Key Issues in Hussite Theology"
  - Rychterová, Pavlína — "Preaching, the Vernacular, and the Laity"
  - **Holeton, David R., Kolář, Pavel, & Baťová, Eliška — "Liturgy, Sacramental Theology, and Music"** (this is Holeton's last collaborative chapter on the topic — directly load-bearing for §4.1 and §5)
  - Halama, Ota — "The Unity of Brethren (1458–1496)"
  - **Haberkern, Phillip — "The Bohemian Reformation and 'The' Reformation: Hussites and Protestants in Early Modern Europe"** (the chapter most directly engages the Utraquist→Lutheran reception thread)
- Relevance: §1, §2, §3, §4.1, §4.2, §5.

#### 2.14 Šmahel, František (ed.), with Pavlíček, Ota — *A Companion to Jan Hus*. Brill's Companions to the Christian Tradition 54. Leiden/Boston: Brill, 2015. x+447 pp. ISBN 9789004280557. $210 cloth.
- [Brill page](https://brill.com/edcollbook/title/18188)
- **Signature:** Predecessor companion volume — Hus rather than the broader Hussites. **Holeton's chapter "Second Life of Jan Hus: Liturgy, Commemoration & Music" (with Vlhová-Wörner)** is the most thoroughly researched Anglophone treatment of Hus liturgical commemoration — directly relevant for §5 (Hus reception in CS).
- Relevance: §3, §5.

## §4 — Genuine gaps

These are topics or authors expected but where Anglophone literature is scant or absent.

### 4.1 The "Lutheranisation of Czech hymn material" Anglophone gap

Despite intensive search, **there is no English-language monograph or major article that directly studies the dogmatic re-coding that occurs as Czech Reformation hymns enter Slovak Lutheran usage.** Wilkinson (3.63/3.64) is the closest, but at thesis-overview pitch, not detailed locus-by-locus. Atwood touches the issue briefly via the Weisse 1531 transmission case (`Crews_2018` already documents this from a Brethren angle). **This is the chapter's contribution-niche.** Holeton's planned (apparently unwritten) Sweden–Bohemia comparative-liturgy monograph would have been the closest analogue.

### 4.2 Anglophone work on Czech Reformation reception of patristic theology

The chapter's §4.4 (Authority of Scripture and Tradition) needs Czech Reformation engagement with patristic sources. Anglophone scholarship is thin: Atwood touches it briefly; David's BRRP essays gesture at it in passing; no monograph exists. (Czech-language: Halama's *Otázka svatých v české reformaci* exists but isn't translated.) **Gap to acknowledge in chapter, not to fill.**

### 4.3 Anglophone work on the Bystrická agenda (1585) specifically

Apart from the Benka 2024 critical edition (already in index) and Chalupský 2024 (already in index), there is no Anglophone article-length treatment. **Acceptable gap** — Czech-Slovak language scholarship is the natural site for this question, and Slovak/Czech sources are well-documented in the index.

### 4.4 Comparative Anglophone monographic work on Anglican–Utraquist parallels (David's repeated theme)

Zdeněk V. David has pursued the Anglican–Utraquist via media analogy across at least four BRRP essays (3.13, 3.14, 3.15) and `David_1999`. **He never wrote the monograph** — the comparative-Anglican monograph that would parallel his *Finding the Middle Way*. This is a methodologically significant absence for the chapter, since the Slovak Lutheran reception thread is in some respects a parallel project: how a Reformation church absorbed (rather than displaced) earlier reform.

### 4.5 Utraquist-Lutheran comparative-Eucharistic monograph

Burnett (3.24) is the closest, but treats Hussite influence on the *Karlstadt-Zwingli* track, not the *Wittenberg-Slovak* track. **Genuine gap, methodologically significant for §4.1.** No author has written this book.

### 4.6 Holeton's unfinished work

The planned "Late Sixteenth Century Latin Liturgies of Lutheran Sweden and Utraquist Bohemia" never appeared. **Verify with Vlhová-Wörner** (or with the Hussite Theological Faculty at Charles Univ.) whether Holeton's drafts/notes survive and might be edited posthumously.
