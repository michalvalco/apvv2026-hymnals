### `Crews_2018` original *Hinge* citation
- The bibliographic anomaly flagged in `Crews_2018.md` (volume/issue 14:1 vs. 12:3 for the 2005 *Hinge* reprint) **remains unresolved** by my searches. The 2018 JMH editor's note prints "14, no. 1 (2005): 21–54"; the Moravian Theological Seminary bibliography prints "12:3 (2005), 21–54"; Wikipedia agrees with 12:3. **Both agree on year and pagination.** The 12:3 numbering is the more frequently cited form in third-party sources. Recommend citing JMH 2018 directly and noting the *Hinge* original only when its 1997-lecture provenance is load-bearing.

- The 2009 book contains a substantial chapter on Brethren Eucharistic theology that complements Crews 2018 with sustained attention to the early-Unity → Lukáš shift in real-presence formulation. The Atwood-Crews relationship is **complementary, not redundant**: Atwood is more synthetic across the whole arc; Crews is denser on Lukáš specifically.

## 2. NEW Essential items (15 entries)

### Anglophone — Brethren scholarship

#### Atwood, Craig D. — "Catechism of the Bohemian Brethren, Translated and Edited from the 1523 German Version" (2007)
- **Citation:** *Journal of Moravian History* 2, no. 1 (April 2007): 91–117.
- **URL:** https://scholarlypublishingcollective.org/psup/moravian-history/article-abstract/2/1/91/303700/
- **Signature:** Atwood's translation of the 1523 German printing of Lukáš Pražský's catechism — the very document Crews 2018 (pp. 35–36) names but does not provide. Twenty-six pages of primary text in modern English.
- **§-relevance:** §4.4 (Scripture/tradition — catechism as ministrative locus); §4.1 (sacramental questions in the catechism); §5 (the German version travelled with Weisse 1531 hymnody — same Czech → German channel that pre-figures the Slovak Lutheran reception). **Essential** because no other complete English translation of any pre-1535 Brethren catechism exists.

#### Atwood, Craig D. — "The Bohemian Brethren and the Protestant Reformation" (2021)
- **Citation:** *Religions* 12, no. 5 (2021): 360. DOI: 10.3390/rel12050360.
- **Open access PDF:** https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/c4cc/9d212425455e721c36a716302a01a58593b7.pdf
- **MDPI page:** https://www.mdpi.com/2077-1444/12/5/360
- **Signature:** Atwood's mature synthesis on how Brethren theology entered the Protestant Reformation — irenic, ecumenical, voluntary-church model. Frames the Roh-Weisse 1522 visit to Wittenberg as the Brethren's *introduction* of their theology to Lutheranism (not the reverse).
- **§-relevance:** §3 (Brethren as senior partner, not junior, in 1520s Lutheran-Brethren contact — disciplines our framing of the directional reception thesis); §4.2 (voluntary-church / ecclesiology); §5 (justifies the chapter's emphasis on Brethren-content-flowing-into-Lutheran-frameworks rather than the reverse). **Essential** — the most recent Atwood synthesis and the one that reframes the Roh/Weisse-Wittenberg encounter most clearly.

#### Atwood, Craig D. — "The Use of the 'Ancient Unity' in the Historiography of the Moravian Church" (2013)
- **Citation:** *Journal of Moravian History* 13, no. 2 (Fall 2013): 109–157 (page range tentative — verify on first chapter use).
- **Project MUSE summary:** https://muse.jhu.edu/article/526097/summary
- **Semantic Scholar:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/d6f151d89814cc7fa9667e41bb19b7c64f05219f
- **Signature:** Methodologically vital. Examines how 18th–19th-c. Moravians (including Edmund de Schweinitz) constructed the "Ancient Unity" as a usable past. Discusses the "Hidden Seed" myth.
- **§-relevance:** §1 / §3 — discipline against teleological readings of the Brethren-to-Moravian-to-Pietist line; the chapter's pre-1700 cut-off is principled, not arbitrary, and Atwood here documents *why* the Herrnhut-renewal claim of continuity is itself a 19th-c. confessional construction. **Essential** for chapter introduction's framing of Brethren tradition as a discrete pre-1700 object.

#### Crews, C. Daniel — *Faith, Love, Hope: A History of the Unitas Fratrum* (2008)
- **Citation:** Winston-Salem, NC: Moravian Archives, 2008. ISBN 978-0-9719411-3-7.
- **Publisher:** https://moravianarchives.org/product/faith-love-hope-a-history-of-the-unitas-fratrum/
- **JMH review (2009):** https://scholarlypublishingcollective.org/psup/moravian-history/article/9/1/123/303794/
- **Signature:** Crews's own narrative history of the Unity from 1457 to the early modern period, written from his archival vantage. Less dense than Crews 2018 article on Lukáš, but covers the *whole* pre-Pietist Unity narrative in English.
- **§-relevance:** §1 (introduction-level overview), §3 (Brethren confessional context), §4.2 (institutional history and ecclesiology). **Essential** because it is the only book-length English-language history of the Unity from a major archivist after the 1992 Crews translation of Říčan, and is methodologically lighter than Atwood 2009 but more accessible.

#### Crews, C. Daniel — "The Theology of John Hus with Special Reference to His Concepts of Salvation" (1975)
- **Citation:** PhD dissertation, University of Manchester, 1975. Unpublished.
- **Acquisition:** ProQuest Dissertations & Theses Global; or interlibrary loan via Manchester.
- **Signature:** Crews's foundational scholarship on Hus's soteriology, written 23 years before his Lukáš lecture. The dissertation grounds Crews's authority on Bohemian theology and gives the Hus-Lukáš continuity argument its scholarly substrate.
- **§-relevance:** §3 (Brethren claim Hus as antecedent, but on what theological grounds — Crews's dissertation provides the answer); §4.3 (Hus's soteriology vs. Brethren *Bárka* 1493 vs. Lutheran *sola fide*). **Essential** for chapter claims about the Hus → Brethren soteriological continuity that Crews's Luke article presupposes but does not argue.

#### Wagner, Murray L. — *Petr Chelčický: A Radical Separatist in Hussite Bohemia* (1983)
- **Citation:** Studies in Anabaptist and Mennonite History 25. Scottdale, PA / Kitchener, ON: Herald Press, 1983. ISBN 0-8361-1257-1.
- **Open-access translation of *Sieť viery*:** https://www.elcristianismoprimitivo.com/Net_of_Faith.pdf
- **Goodreads:** https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/3830419-petr-chel-ick
- **Signature:** Standard Anglophone monograph on Chelčický, the Brethren's pre-eponymous source. Anabaptist-Mennonite scholarly framing — places Chelčický in radical-reformation context.
- **§-relevance:** §3 (Chelčický's pacifist-separatist theology as the substrate the Lutheran reception deliberately *excises* when Brethren material enters CS); §4.2 (the radical-ecclesiology element of Brethren tradition that Slovak Lutheran reception filters out). **Essential** for any chapter claim about what Slovak Lutheran reception of Brethren material *omits*.

#### Schweinitz, Edmund de — *The History of the Church Known as the Unitas Fratrum, or the Unity of the Brethren, Founded by the Followers of John Hus, the Bohemian Reformer and Martyr* (1885)
- **Citation:** Bethlehem, PA: Moravian Publications Office, 1885. 712 pp.
- **Free on Archive.org:** https://archive.org/details/historyofchurchk00desc_0
- **Full-text version:** https://archive.org/stream/historyofchurchk00desc_1/historyofchurchk00desc_1_djvu.txt
- **Catalog record (HathiTrust):** https://catalog.hathitrust.org/Record/006773706
- **Signature:** The 19th-c. foundational English-language Brethren history. Out of copyright, complete on Archive.org. Pre-Říčan, pre-Crews — represents the older Anglophone-Moravian framing that Atwood 2013 critiques.
- **§-relevance:** §1 (Anglophone scholarly tradition's older framing); §3 (institutional history). **Essential** as the historical anchor of Anglophone Brethren scholarship; should be cited where the chapter notes Anglophone framings of the Brethren tradition.

#### Schweinitz, Edmund de (trans.) — "The Catechism of the Bohemian Brethren. Translated from the Old German, with an Introduction" (1893)
- **Citation:** *Transactions of the Moravian Historical Society* (issue/pages tbc on first chapter use).
- **JSTOR:** https://www.jstor.org/stable/41179521
- **Open-access scan:** https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0b/The_Catechism_of_the_Bohemian_Brethren._Translated_from_the_old_German,_with_an_Introduction_(IA_jstor-41179521).pdf

### Czech-language — Brethren scholarship

#### Halama, Jindřich — *Sociální učení Českých bratří 1464–1618* (2003)
- **Citation:** Brno: Centrum pro studium demokracie a kultury (CDK), 2003. (Habilitation thesis.)
- **German trans.:** *Die Soziallehre der Böhmischen Brüder 1464 bis 1618.* Herrnhut: Herrnhuter Verlag, 2017.
- **Author page:** https://web.etf.cuni.cz/ETFENG-190.html
- **Catalogue:** https://www.databazeknih.cz/knihy/socialni-uceni-ceskych-bratri-1464-1618-200418
- **Signature:** The major contemporary Czech-language monograph on Brethren social ethics across the entire Unity period (1464–1618). Halama's thesis: Brethren social teaching remained an alternative model throughout, distinct from both mainstream Czech society and from other Reformation churches. He is currently active at ETF UK and has published on Brethren-related ethics elsewhere.
- **§-relevance:** §4.3 (Brethren ethics — vital for grasping what the Slovak Lutheran reception filters out when CS absorbs Brethren material); §3 (Brethren social model as confessional marker). **Essential** — pairs naturally with Crews 2018 and Foušek 1961 to give the chapter the Czech-language ethical-social register. The 2017 German translation makes it accessible to readers without Czech.

#### Landová, Tabita — "Ein fröhliches Mahl: Die Abendmahlsliturgie der Brüderunität" (2011)
- **Citation:** *Communio Viatorum* 53, no. 2 (2011): 6–29.
- **Author page (with publication list):** https://web.etf.cuni.cz/ETFENG-203.html
- **Signature:** Landová's German-language article on the Brethren Eucharistic liturgy — directly relevant to §4.1, written for an international scholarly audience, and the most accessible single article-length entry into her 2014 monograph's argument.
- **§-relevance:** §4.1 (Brethren Eucharistic rite vs. Lutheran rite vs. Utraquist Agenda Česká rite — the three-way comparison central to §4.1). **Essential** — German-language access to Landová's specifically Eucharistic argument; sits alongside Peschke 1935 in establishing the German-language Brethren-Eucharist dossier.

#### Landová, Tabita — "Motiv oběti v eucharistické liturgii Jednoty bratrské" (2010)
- **Citation:** *Religio* 18, no. 1 (2010): 85–101.
- **Signature:** Landová on the sacrificial motif in Brethren Eucharistic liturgy. Czech-language; relevant precisely because the Lutheran reception's anti-sacrificial polemic (cf. Luther *De captivitate* 1520, `Luther_DeCaptiv_1520`) maps directly onto what Landová describes.
- **§-relevance:** §4.1 (sacrifice / sacramental theology — the Brethren *do* have sacrificial language in their Eucharistic rite, contra a simple "memorialist" reading; this complicates the Lutheran reception story). **Essential**.

#### Landová, Tabita — "Křest jako smlouva: Křestní liturgie Jednoty bratrské za Lukáše Pražského" (2011)
- **Citation:** *Teologická reflexe* 17, no. 1 (2011): 49–65.
- **Signature:** Brethren baptismal rite under Lukáš Pražský, on the covenantal-baptismal model. Pairs methodologically with `Chalupsky_2024` (Agenda Česká baptismal rite) for cross-confessional comparison.
- **§-relevance:** §4.1 (sacraments — baptism specifically); §3 (the rebaptism issue Crews 2018 names as one of Luther's four disagreements with Lukáš). **Essential** because the chapter's §4.1 needs Brethren baptismal data and Landová is the canonical contemporary source.

#### Landová, Tabita — *O službě slova, víře a spasení: Reformní kazatelství Jana Augusty v kontextu homiletiky Jednoty bratrské* (2021)
- **Citation:** Praha: Karolinum, 2021. 294 pp. ISBN 978-80-246-4750-0.
- **Signature:** Landová's second monograph: Jan Augusta's reform preaching in Brethren homiletical context. Augusta is a key successor to Lukáš and the figure who pushed Brethren rapprochement with Wittenberg in the 1530s–1540s.
- **§-relevance:** §3 (the post-Lukáš generation that *did* engage Lutheran theology); §4.4 (preaching as ministrative locus); §5 (homiletical-textual transmission patterns parallel to hymnological patterns). **Essential** for any chapter claim about post-1528 (post-Lukáš) Brethren-Lutheran rapprochement.

#### Vlhová-Wörner, Hana — Editions and articles on Bohemian-Reformation chant and Jistebnický kancionál
- **Affiliation:** University of Basel and Masaryk Institute, AV ČR Praha. https://www.mua.cas.cz/en/lide/detail-vlhova-worner
- **Major edition (with Holeton et al.):** *Jistebnický kancionál*, vol. 1 *Graduale*, ed. Jaroslav Kolár, Anežka Vidmanová, Hana Vlhová-Wörner. Brno: L. Marek, 2005. (Critical edition cited in `KancJisteb_1420` already; her authorship of the Jistebnický apparatus warrants a Section V entry.)
- **Recent contribution:** "Medieval Traditions of Plainchant in Bohemia," chapter in *A History of Music in the Czech Lands* (Cambridge UP, 2025).
- **Signature:** The principal editor of the Jistebnický kancionál critical edition, who also writes general histories of Bohemian-Reformation chant. Direct continuity with the Holeton-Wörner editorial axis on the Bohemian liturgical-chant primary sources.
- **§-relevance:** §3 (Bohemian-Reformation chant tradition that fed Brethren and Utraquist hymnody alike); §4.1 (chant proper of the Brethren rite). **Essential** as the contemporary musicological-philological authority on the pre-1500 chant substrate that both Brethren and Utraquist hymnody draw from, and which the Czech sources transmitted into Slovak Lutheran usage carry as residue.

## Surprising / Notable Discoveries

1. **Halama 2003 / 2017** (J. Halama on Brethren social teaching) is the largest single Czech-language Brethren-scholarship gap in `Source_Index.md`. The German translation makes it accessible. Pair with `Fousek_1961` and `Crews_2018` for the §4.3 ethics dossier.

2. **Atwood 2021** is freely accessible (open-access *Religions*) and reframes the Brethren-Lutheran encounter in exactly the directional way the chapter wants. This is the single highest-yield zero-cost pickup in the survey.

3. **Landová's article-length output** is much larger than the 2014 monograph alone. Her 2010–2021 articles distil the monograph's specific arguments (Eucharist 2010/2011, baptism 2011, ecclesiology 2021) into journal-paper form, making her work much more practical to cite than I had assumed before this survey.

4. **The Vogt 2018 / Crews 2018 pairing in JMH 18/1** is a one-issue treasury for the chapter: Crews on Lukáš (already documented), Vogt on Brethren-Moravian terminology, and the issue's editorial framing helps justify the chapter's pre-1700 cut-off.

5. **The Acta Unitatis Fratrum critical-edition project** (Bahlcke / Halama / Holý / Just / Rothkegel / Udolph, 2018+) is currently in progress and will eventually displace `ActaUF` references to badatelna.eu/fond/1014. Track future volumes.

6. **The Molnár bibliography is far larger than `Source_Index.md` reflects.** Molnár wrote ~50 articles directly on Brethren / Hus / Lukáš topics from 1948 to 1989, in five languages (Czech, French, German, Italian, Slovak), spread across Communio Viatorum, Křesťanská revue, Theologická příloha, Revue d'Histoire et de Philosophie Religieuses, Bollettino della Società di Studi Valdesi, and others. The Source_Index entry for `Molnar_1948` covers the monograph; Michal should consider a separate "Molnár articles" cross-reference accumulation as the chapter develops, especially the French-language CV pieces ("Luc de Prague et Constantinople" 1961; "Luc de Prague devant la crise de l'Unité" 1961; "Pasteur dans la tourmente" 1963) and the German-language "Luther und die Böhmischen Brüder" *CV* 24 (1981): 47–67 — directly load-bearing for §3.
