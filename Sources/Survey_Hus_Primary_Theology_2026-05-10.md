# Survey — Hus Primary Editions and Theological-Textual Scholarship

**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Scope:** Phase 1 reconnaissance for the directional-reception thesis. Half A locates Hus / Utraquist primary critical editions not yet flagged in `Sources/Source_Index.md`; Half B locates theological-textual scholarship beyond the biographical narrative already represented locally by Fudge 2010, Masaryk 1896, Flajshans 1915, Ligus 2017.
**Method:** Iterative searches against MIHOO/Brepols (Corpus Christianorum), brrp.org, Manuscriptorium, JSTOR/Cambridge Core/Brill, university repositories, openedition.org, Karolinum, Filosofia.
**Triage:** Per project instructions: *Essential* for items load-bearing on the four loci or on the directional-reception argument; *Secondary* for contextual Hussitology; *Excluded* (not listed) for items duplicating Fudge / Masaryk / Flajshans biographical territory.

The single most important framing finding: **the Brno-based Magistri Iohannis Hus Opera Omnia (MIHOO) project is now hosted in Brepols's *Corpus Christianorum Continuatio Mediaevalis* subseries, and the older Czech Academy editions (1959–1988) have been progressively reissued and supplemented since 2004**. Several of the texts the brief listed as targets (*De ecclesia*, *De corpore Cristi*, *De sanguine Cristi glorificato*, *De officio sacerdotis*) are explicitly **announced as in preparation but not yet published** in the MIHOO/CCCM series — meaning the older Thomson 1956 (for *De ecclesia*) and Daňhelka 1980s editions remain the working standard in the absence of the modern critical edition. This is itself a finding the chapter needs to flag.

## Section 1 — Half A: Primary Editions Verified or Newly Located

### 1.1 The MIHOO/CCCM master series (umbrella entry)

**Magistri Iohannis Hus Opera Omnia (MIHOO).** Hosted at Centre for Medieval Studies, Masaryk University Brno (https://hus.phil.muni.cz/) in cooperation with Brepols. Project incorporated into Corpus Christianorum Continuatio Mediaevalis from 2004 (https://www.corpuschristianorum.org/cccm-hus). Plan: 26 tomi. Volumes published since 2004 each retain their original MIHOO tomus number AND receive a CCCM volume number. Database of all Hus's works available at https://mihoo.phil.muni.cz/.

This is the **master umbrella** — Half A entries 1.2–1.7 below are individual MIHOO volumes documented separately because the chapter will cite specific texts, but for a single bibliography line item the MIHOO series itself should be acknowledged as the primary scholarly infrastructure.

**Status:** ESSENTIAL (umbrella reference). Found via: search "Magistri Iohannis Hus Opera Omnia" + Brepols CCCM. Connection: §3 (dogmatic profiles), §4.1, §4.2 (any direct Hus citation). Not yet in Source_Index.md.

### 1.2 Hus, *Constantiensia* (CCCM 274 = MIHOO XXIV) — 2016

**Full reference:** Helena Krmíčková, Jana Nechutová et al. (eds.), *Magistri Iohannis Hus Constantiensia* (Corpus Christianorum Continuatio Mediaevalis 274 = MIHOO XXIV). Turnhout: Brepols, 2016. ISBN-13 9782503554693. Brepols product page: https://www.brepols.net/products/IS-9782503554693-1.

**What it contains:** the cluster of treatises Hus wrote in the months leading up to Constance and during his imprisonment there: *Sermo de pace*, *De sufficiencia legis Cristi*, *De fidei sue elucidacione*, ***De sanguine Cristi sub specie vini*** (Hus's direct word on the lay chalice question), *Deposiciones testium*, *Responsiones ad articulos Wiclef*, *Responsiones ad articulos Palecz*. Reviewed in *Mediaevalia* 34/1 (2021) (Aaron Vanides) — "a great leap forward for research on Hus and the Council of Constance." Editorial team led by Krmíčková (Brno), continuing work originally begun by Amedeo Molnár.

**Status:** ESSENTIAL — supersedes any earlier edition of the Constance treatises. NOT yet in `Source_Index.md`. Particularly load-bearing because *De sanguine Cristi sub specie vini* is the Hus-side counterpart to Jakoubek's *Salvator Noster* — the two sub utraque defences that Patapios 2002 (already flagged) compared. **Connection:** §4.1 (Eucharistic locus, sub utraque controversy), §4.4 (authority of Scripture and patristic citation in Hus's late argument).
**Relevance:** Directly serves the lay-chalice strand of the §4.1 comparative analysis with the most authoritative critical text available.

### 1.3 Hus, *Postilla adumbrata* (Latin sermons) — Ryba/Silagi edition

**Full reference:** Bohumil Ryba (ed.), revised by Gabriel Silagi, *Magistri Iohannis Hus Postilla adumbrata* (MIHOO XIII = CCCM 261). Turnhout: Brepols, 2014 (revised edition of Ryba's 1975 Czech Academy text).

**What it is:** Hus's Latin reworking of his Czech vernacular Postilla, attested in Bohemian manuscripts; functioned as a preacher's resource and Hussite catechetical anchor. **Distinct** from the Czech *Postilla* (see 1.4). Reviewed widely in Czech Reformation literature.

**Status:** ESSENTIAL. Found via: Brepols / MIHOO listings. NOT in `Source_Index.md`. **Connection:** §4.4 (Hus's Scripture-and-tradition method via sermon exegesis); §3 (Hus's pastoral-theological profile).

### 1.4 Hus, *Česká nedělní postila* (Czech Sunday Postil) — Daňhelka 1992

**Full reference:** Jiří Daňhelka (ed.), *Mistr Jan Hus: Česká nedělní postila — Vyloženie svatých čtení nedělních*. Praha: Academia (Academia Scientiarum Bohemoslovaca), 1992. Volume of the *Magistri Iohannis Hus Opera omnia* Czech-language tomus series predating the Brepols incorporation.

**What it is:** the 1413 vernacular Sunday-readings postil that Hus composed at Kozí Hrádek after his Prague exile — Daňhelka characterised it as Hus's "most important and most personal" Czech work (search confirmation 2026-05-10). The vernacular postil is a different text from the Latin *Postilla adumbrata* (1.3), and has had documentable independent reception (cf. Odstrčilík at OEAW on Latin re-translation). **Manuscripts:** Brno, Moravian Regional Library Mk56 and Mk91.

**Status:** ESSENTIAL. NOT in `Source_Index.md`. **Connection:** §4.3 (vernacular formation of moral-pastoral programme), §4.4 (vernacular Scripture in pre-Reformation Bohemia — anchors a strand the chapter shares with `Hrobon_2011`/`Kyas_1997` on Czech-Bible history). **Acquisition:** print-only; ILL Charles University or Czech antikvariát.

### 1.5 Hus, *Knížky o svatokupectví* (On Simony) — Daňhelka

**Full reference:** Jiří Daňhelka (ed.), in *Mistr Jan Hus: Drobné spisy české / Sebrané spisy* (Praha: Academia, 1980s — verify exact volume). Earlier editions: V. Novotný (1907), A. Gregor (1954). Online vernacular edition with apparatus available at vokabular.ujc.cas.cz (Czech Language Institute critical-edition module).

**Status:** ESSENTIAL. NOT in `Source_Index.md`. **Connection:** §4.2 (ecclesiology — Hus's anti-clerical reform programme); §4.3 (ethics of clerical office). The simony tractate is the load-bearing text for any directional argument that Slovak Lutheran reception of Hus picked up the moral-ecclesiological strand alongside (or rather than) the philosophical-realist strand. **Online text:** https://vokabular.ujc.cas.cz/moduly/edicni/edice/b0b6fef9-fa7d-48b6-949d-831ca4a9819d/plny-text/s-aparatem/folio/0.

### 1.6 Hus, *Knížky proti knězi kuchmistrovi* — Daňhelka

**Full reference:** Jiří Daňhelka (ed.), *Mistr Jan Hus: Drobné spisy české*, MIHOO IV (Praha: Academia, **1985**). [**RESOLVED 2026-05-10** per `Survey_URLcheck_2026-05-10.md`; the original "1980s — confirm exact volume" hedge is superseded.] Polemical Czech tractate from 1414 against an apostate-priest-turned-cook. Brief but vivid; useful sample of Hus's Czech vernacular polemical register.

**Status:** SECONDARY (not load-bearing for any single locus, but represents Hus's late vernacular polemical voice). NOT in `Source_Index.md`. **Connection:** §3 (Hus's profile and tone) only.

### 1.7 Hus, *Letters from Constance / Korespondence* — Spinka 1972 EN translation

**Full reference:** Matthew Spinka (trans. and ed.), *The Letters of John Hus*. Manchester: Manchester University Press, 1972. ISBN-10 0719005175 / 0874710219 (US edition, Iowa State UP). 105 letters, translated from Latin and Czech. Available second-hand widely; print only.

**What it is:** the standard English-language translation of Hus's complete extant correspondence including the prison letters from Constance (Nov 1414 – July 1415). Spinka also produced *John Hus at the Council of Constance* (Columbia 1965), a separate translation-with-commentary of the Constance proceedings. The Czech-language scholarly edition for the letters proper is now superseded by the MIHOO `Constantiensia` (1.2 above) for the Latin treatises composed at Constance, but the **letters** as such still rely on Novotný's *Korrespondence a dokumenty Mistra Jana Husi* (1920).

**Status:** ESSENTIAL (English-language access to Hus's prison voice). NOT in `Source_Index.md`. **Connection:** §3 (Hus profile and witness), §4.4 (Hus on Scripture and conscience under interrogation — the locus where the chapter's authority-of-Scripture argument intersects with Hus's biography).
**Relevance:** The Spinka translation is the only complete English-language reference for Hus's epistolary corpus; Slovak Lutherans encountered Hus through reception traditions, but for English-language scholarship the chapter must position the modern reader.

### 1.8 Matěj of Janov, *Regulae Veteris et Novi Testamenti* — Kybal/Odložilík + Nechutová 1993

**Full reference:**
- Older edition (foundational, complete): Vlastimil Kybal & Otakar Odložilík (eds.), *Matthiae de Janov dicti Magistri Parisiensis Regulae Veteris et Novi Testamenti*. Innsbruck/Praha, 1908–1926. Five volumes. Available on Internet Archive: https://archive.org/details/p5matthiaedejanovr06matjuoft (Vol. 5/6, *De corpore Cristi*).
- Modern critical edition (Book V only, with German introduction and apparatus): Jana Nechutová (ed.), *Matthiae de Janov dicti Magistri Parisiensis Regularum Veteris et Novi Testamenti, Liber V: De corpore Cristi*. München: R. Oldenbourg Verlag, 1993 (Veröffentlichungen des Collegium Carolinum vol. 73).

**What it is:** the **immediate doctrinal source** of Jakoubek's lay-chalice argument per Krmíčková BRRP3 (already flagged). Nechutová's separately edited Book V (specifically on the Body of Christ / lay communion) is the volume directly relevant to the §4.1 sacramental locus.

**Important caveat:** there is **no CCCM Brepols critical edition** of the *Regulae* despite the brief's expectation — the Kybal/Odložilík text remains the standard for Books I–IV and VI, with Nechutová 1993 the critical text for Book V. Nechutová also published "Matěj of Janov: Corpus Mysticum, Communionem, and the Lost Treatise of His *Regulae*," *Religions* 9/1 (2018) — open access at https://www.mdpi.com/2077-1444/9/1/16 — for the most recent state of the question.

**Status:** ESSENTIAL. NOT in `Source_Index.md` despite multiple references to Janovite theory throughout the existing index. **Connection:** §4.1 (sacramental theology — the deepest stratum of the lay-chalice argument), §4.4 (biblicism in Janov as anti-clerical impulse).
**Relevance:** Without Janov 1908/1993 directly accessible the chapter cannot do more than echo Krmíčková on the chain Janov → Jakoubek → Hus → Utraquist liturgical practice.

### 1.9 Jakoubek of Stříbro — beyond the V-M 017 entry

The existing `Jakoubek_Euch` entry already captures the Tábor V-M 017 manuscript and the principal Eucharistic writings. **Newly located, not yet flagged:**

**Full reference:** Helena Krmíčková, "Articulus pro communione sub utraque specie Jakoubka ze Stříbra," *Studie o rukopisech* 39/1 (2010), pp. 73–87. Open access at https://asjournals.lib.cas.cz/Studieorukopisech/. Includes Krmíčková's edition of a short Jakoubek treatise + her demonstration that the Pauline 1 Cor 11:28 ("probet autem se ipsum homo") was the decisive scriptural anchor and confirmed Janov-via-Jakoubek lineage.

**Status:** ESSENTIAL — short, but a critical-edition + analysis fusion that the chapter can cite without needing to access the manuscript directly. NOT in `Source_Index.md`. **Connection:** §4.1 (sub utraque defence narrative), §4.4 (Pauline anchor for the lay chalice).

### 1.10 Hus's *De ecclesia* — note on edition status

The existing `Hus_DeEccl_1413` entry points to the 1958 Thomson edition and Schaff's 1915 EN translation. **MIHOO has *De ecclesia* in preparation but not yet published** (confirmed via corpuschristianorum.org/cccm-hus). The Thomson 1958 (S. Harrison Thomson, Cambridge) remains the **current standard critical text**; the Brepols/MIHOO replacement is anticipated but should not be cited as if available. This is a state-of-edition note for `Source_Index.md`, not a new entry.

### 2.2 Haberkern, Phillip N., *Patron Saint and Prophet: Jan Hus in the Bohemian and German Reformations* (Oxford 2016)

**Full reference:** Phillip N. Haberkern, *Patron Saint and Prophet: Jan Hus in the Bohemian and German Reformations*. Oxford Studies in Historical Theology. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2016. xii + 334 pp. ISBN-13 978-0-19-028073-4. $78 cloth. Available also via Oxford Academic (https://academic.oup.com/book/26934). Reviewed *AHR* 123/1 (2018); *Journal of Religion* 99/1 (2019).

**What it is:** the **definitive English-language monograph on Hus's reception**, tracing his memorial transmission from 1415 through the 1550s — explicitly across three audiences: the militant Hussite movement, the Czech Utraquist church, and **sixteenth-century Lutherans who saw him as forerunner and prophet** (this last is the chapter's exact target). Methodology integrates sermons, church histories, manuscript illuminations, monumental art, **vernacular songs, and liturgical prayers** — the same *lex orandi* attentiveness the chapter uses.

**Status:** ESSENTIAL — most load-bearing single secondary acquisition after the Brill Companion. NOT in `Source_Index.md`. **Connection:** §5 (Slovak Lutheran reception), §3 (broader Bohemian/German confessional reception of Hus), but **especially the methodological-precedent function**: Haberkern uses the same hymn-and-liturgy-as-theological-witness method the chapter argues for; cite him as methodological precedent.
**Relevance:** This is the book the chapter's reception-of-Hus thread should be in conversation with throughout.

### 2.3 Halama, Ota, *Svatý Jan Hus: Stručný přehled projevů domácí úcty k českému mučedníku v letech 1415–1620* (Praha: Kalich, 2015)

**Full reference:** Ota Halama, *Svatý Jan Hus: Stručný přehled projevů domácí úcty k českému mučedníku v letech 1415–1620*. Praha: Kalich, 2015. Reviewed (with academia.edu PDF) at https://www.academia.edu/39060324/.

**What it is:** the Czech-language counterpart to Haberkern (2.2), on Hus's domestic Czech veneration 1415–1620. Halama's particular contribution is showing that the **veneration of Hus** (alongside the lay chalice) was the principal identity-forming axis for the entirety of Czech Protestantism (Hussites, Utraquists, Brethren, and Lutherans). For the chapter, Halama provides the Czech-side narrative against which the Slovak Lutheran reception (Tranovský and after) can be measured — i.e., how much of CS's Hus-related material represents continuation vs. de-veneration vs. re-positioning of the inherited Czech cult of Hus.

**Status:** ESSENTIAL. NOT in `Source_Index.md`. **Connection:** §5 — directly load-bearing for the "Lutheranisation of Czech Hus-veneration" sub-argument. Pairs naturally with Haberkern (2.2). Acquisition: Czech bookshop or Charles University Press.
**Note on potential overlap:** Haberkern's German-Reformation chapters and Halama's Czech-domestic narrative do not duplicate; they complement.

### 2.4 Halama, Ota, *Otázka svatých v české reformaci* (Brno: L. Marek, 2002, Pontes Pragenses 19)

**Full reference:** Ota Halama, *Otázka svatých v české reformaci: Její proměny od doby Karla IV. do doby České konfese*. Brno: L. Marek, 2002. Pontes Pragenses 19. Based on his 1999–2001 ETF UK dissertation.

**What it is:** the foundational study of how the Czech Reformation handled the cult of saints across two centuries. Engages directly with Matěj of Janov, the Hussite Revolution, Rokycana's compromise, Utraquist church practice, and the Unity of Brethren. Halama corrects the standard simplification that "Hussite reform = saint-rejection + iconoclasm": Janov rejected, but the question faded from Hussite ideology, and most Hussite theologians rejected saint cults without iconoclasm.

**Status:** ESSENTIAL — uniquely positioned for the §4.2-into-§5 question of how saint-veneration translated into hymnody (engages directly with the same territory `GresovaSek_2019` (CS *De Cultu Sanctorum*) tracks on the Slovak side). NOT in `Source_Index.md`. **Connection:** §4.2 (ecclesiology and saint-mediation), §5 (CS hymn corpus on saints — Sekelská 2019 territory).

### 2.5 Soukup, Pavel, *Reformní kazatelství a Jakoubek ze Stříbra* (Praha: Filosofia, 2011)

**Full reference:** Pavel Soukup, *Reformní kazatelství a Jakoubek ze Stříbra* [Reform Preaching and Jakoubek of Stříbro]. Praha: Filosofia, 2011. 438 pp. ISBN 978-80-7007-359-9. Karolinum: https://www.cupress.cuni.cz/ink2_ext/index.jsp?include=podrobnosti&id=218540. English summary at https://www.academia.edu/7542221/.

**What it is:** based on Soukup's 1999–2007 Charles University doctoral thesis. Examines the role of preaching in the Czech reform movement c. 1360–1420, with focal weight on Jakoubek as preacher. Methodologically rich: how preaching method (interpretation + dissemination of God's Word) shaped the reform programme. **Goes beyond Patapios 2002 / Krmíčková BRRP3 / de Vooght 1972** by foregrounding the homiletic register rather than the eucharistic-theological treatise register.

**Status:** ESSENTIAL. NOT in `Source_Index.md`. **Connection:** §4.4 (Scripture-and-tradition through preaching method), §3 (Jakoubek's profile beyond the eucharistic question).
**Acquisition:** Filosofia Press; ILL through Comenius likely viable.

### 2.6 Soukup, Pavel, *Jan Hus: The Life and Death of a Preacher* (Purdue UP, 2019)

**Full reference:** Pavel Soukup, *Jan Hus: The Life and Death of a Preacher*. Central European Studies. West Lafayette, IN: Purdue University Press, 2019. ISBN 978-1-55753-876-5. English-language translation of his 2015 Czech monograph (*Jan Hus*, Praha: Vyšehrad). Sample at https://docs.lib.purdue.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1043&context=purduepress_previews.

**What it is:** The most recent comprehensive English-language scholarly biography of Hus by a current-generation Czech Hussitologist. **Preferable to Fudge 2010 for theology-rich passages**: Soukup is at the Centre for Medieval Studies (Czech Academy), where the MIHOO project is hosted, and his framing places Hus within the reformist-preaching tradition (i.e., *as theologian and preacher*) rather than as social-revolutionary leader (Fudge's emphasis). Pairs naturally with Soukup's 2011 Jakoubek book (2.5) for a coherent Soukup-Hus-Jakoubek-as-preachers methodological frame.

**Status:** ESSENTIAL. NOT in `Source_Index.md`. **Connection:** §3 (Hus profile, theologian-as-preacher framing). Note: this is *not* duplicative of Fudge 2010 even though both are biographies — Soukup's theological emphasis is what the existing inventory lacks.
**Relevance:** the chapter should cite Soukup 2019 for any claim about Hus's preaching theology where the existing inventory currently relies on Fudge.

### 2.7 Lahey, Stephen E., *John Wyclif* (Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2009)

**Full reference:** Stephen E. Lahey, *John Wyclif*. Great Medieval Thinkers. New York / Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2009. ISBN-13 978-0-19-518332-0 (paperback) / 978-0-19-518331-3 (hardback). Reviewed *Project MUSE* (https://muse.jhu.edu/article/378809/pdf).

**Chapter structure:** (1) Wyclif's Life and Work; (2) The Oxford Context of Wyclif's Thought; (3) Wyclif in Oxford: Logic, Metaphysics; (4) **Denying Transubstantiation: Physics, Eucharist, and Apostasy**; (5) The Logic of Scripture; (6) Predestination and the Church; (7) Dominium as Foundation of Wyclif's Political and Ecclesiological Vision; Epilogue; Appendix: Wyclif's *Confessio*.

**Why included despite the project brief's caution:** the brief said "do not focus on Hus's medieval philosophical context (Wyclif, realism) unless it directly serves the Eucharistic / ecclesiological loci." Lahey's chapter 4 (transubstantiation/Eucharist) and chapter 6 (predestination/Church) **do** directly serve §4.1 and §4.2. The chapter need not engage Wyclif's metaphysics whole, but it cannot avoid the Wyclif Eucharistic background to Jakoubek's *De Remanentia* (already noted in `Jakoubek_Euch` notes via Burnett BRRP11) without leaving an explanatory gap.

**Status:** ESSENTIAL (chapters 4 and 6 only; the rest is contextual). NOT in `Source_Index.md`. **Connection:** §4.1 (remanence; Hus and Jakoubek do not endorse Wyclif's metaphysical remanence in the strong form, but the controversy presupposes it), §4.2 (predestinarian ecclesiology — load-bearing for the Hus *De ecclesia* argument).

### 2.8 Šmahel, František, *Die hussitische Revolution I–III* (Hannover: Hahnsche Buchhandlung, 2002 — MGH Schriften 43)

**Full reference:** František Šmahel, *Die hussitische Revolution*. Translated from Czech by Thomas Krzenck. ### 2.9 Marin, Olivier, *L'archevêque, le maître et le dévot: Genèses du mouvement réformateur pragois (années 1360–1419)* (Paris: Honoré Champion, 2005)

### 2.10 Patschovsky, Alexander, "Ekklesiologie bei Johannes Hus" (1989)

**Full reference:** Alexander Patschovsky, "Ekklesiologie bei Johannes Hus," in *Lebenslehren und Weltentwürfe im Übergang vom Mittelalter zur Neuzeit*, ed. H. Boockmann et al. Abhandlungen der Akademie der Wissenschaften in Göttingen, Phil.-Hist. Kl. 3.179. Göttingen: Vandenhoeck & Ruprecht, 1989, pp. 370–399. (Verify exact pagination.)

**What it is:** Patschovsky's authoritative study of Hus's ecclesiology — most often cited in Hus-ecclesiology literature; the German-language counterpart to Lahey on Wyclif's ecclesiology. ## Section 4 — Genuine Gaps

1. **No modern critical edition of *De ecclesia* is yet published.** MIHOO has it in preparation as of 2026; the chapter must continue to cite Thomson 1958 (Cambridge), with a state-of-edition footnote.

2. **No CCCM Brepols critical edition of Janov's *Regulae Veteris et Novi Testamenti*** despite the brief's expectation. Kybal/Odložilík 1908–1926 + Nechutová 1993 (Book V) remain standard.

3. **No MIHOO/CCCM volume yet for *De corpore Cristi* / *De sanguine Cristi glorificato* / *De officio sacerdotis*.** The Daňhelka 1980s editions of the related Czech material plus the older Loserth Latin texts are the working scholarly basis. (The MIHOO `Constantiensia` 2016 covers *De sanguine Cristi sub specie vini* — a different text written at Constance, often confused with the longer *De sanguine Cristi glorificato*.)

4. **No English-language complete translation of Šmahel's *Husitská revoluce***. Scholars working in English must use Šmahel's BRRP articles + the Brill Companion + Kaminsky 1967 (older but still in print).

5. **Anglophone scholarship on Slovak-language reception of Hus is virtually nonexistent.** The English-language tradition (Haberkern 2016, Soukup 2019, Brill Companion 2015) treats Bohemian and German reception in detail; Slovak Lutheran reception has been pursued in Slovak (Vajda 1944, Mocko, Daniel) and rarely engaged in English. **This is the chapter's clear contribution.**

6. **No critical edition of Jakoubek of Stříbro's complete sermons.** V-M 017 (Tábor) and Wrocław BU I Q 278 are digitally accessible but unedited. Soukup 2011 is the closest thing to a comprehensive theological-historical study; Krmíčková's micro-editions (e.g., 2010 *Studie o rukopisech*) chip away at the corpus.

7. **No comprehensive treatment of Hus reception in the Slovak-Lutheran hymn corpus specifically.** Halama 2015 covers domestic Czech reception 1415–1620; Haberkern 2016 covers Bohemian + German Lutheran reception; the Tranovský/CS axis as a *Hus-receptive* corpus has not been systematically studied. Vajda 1944 (`Vajda_1944` already flagged) provides the closest hymn-by-hymn coverage but does not foreground reception-of-Hus as a category. **This is also a chapter contribution opportunity.**

## Section 7 — Three corrections to the project brief's expectations

1. **There is no "Cambridge Companion to Jan Hus (2018)."** The flagship recent multi-author volume is **Šmahel & Pavlíček (eds.), *A Companion to Jan Hus*, Brill 2015** (Companions to the Christian Tradition 54). Lahey contributes the Sentences-Commentary chapter; Šmahel is chief editor. Cite this volume, not a non-existent Cambridge title.

2. **Lahey's *John Wyclif* (2009) is Oxford, not Cambridge.** Great Medieval Thinkers series. Cambridge UP holds Lahey's earlier *Philosophy and Politics in the Thought of John Wyclif* (2003) and his *Wyclif: Trialogus* edition/translation (2013), but the 2009 single-author Wyclif monograph is Oxford.

3. **Šmahel's *Hussite Revolution* exists in German (2002) but no complete English translation.** *Die hussitische Revolution* (Hahnsche Buchhandlung, MGH Schriften 43, 3 vols., 2286 pp.). The brief's "Brill 2007" reference appears to be a confusion. Šmahel's English-language access points are his BRRP articles + the Brill Companion 2015.

These three corrections may save the chapter from misattribution risk in the bibliography.
