## Prompt

TEPALIT chapter, Phase 2 — Source Documentation (Literature Search sub-session). I need to identify and assess **current, peer-reviewed scholarship** that covers the ground my chapter occupies. The goal is to build a stronger secondary-source foundation than what I currently have.

### Context

My chapter is a dogmatic comparison of Lutheranism, Utraquism, and the Unity of Brethren, examined through liturgical and hymnological sources (16th–18th c.). The core argument: the transfer of hymns from Czech Reformation sources into the Slovak Lutheran Cithara Sanctorum (1636) involved systematic theological adaptation — a "Lutheranisation" — that the *lex orandi, lex credendi* lens can expose.

### What I have so far (documented sources)

- **Grešová-Sekelská 2023** — musicological critical edition of 8 CS hymns. Excellent but focused on melody, not theology.
- **Grešová-Sekelská, Príspevok** — on CS origins (being documented now).
- **Benka 2024** — critical edition of Agenda Česká 1581 + Bystrická agenda 1585 (Monumenta Linguae Slovacae X). Outstanding — includes 80-pp. historical study with Eucharistic theology discussion.
- **Vajda 1944** — B.Div. thesis from Concordia Seminary. The only comprehensive English-language CS history, but it's an 80-year-old student work relying mainly on Mocko (1909) and Haan (1875). Useful as a reference map, not as authoritative scholarship.

### What I need you to search for

Run targeted academic searches for the following categories. For each hit, give me: full citation, a brief assessment of relevance to my chapter, and where to access it (DOI, JSTOR, open access, library).

**Category 1: Cithara Sanctorum scholarship (post-2000)**
- Any monograph, article, or dissertation on the CS published since 2000
- Especially anything on Tranovský's editorial method, his theological filtering of sources, the confessional character of the CS
- Check: Grešová-Sekelská's other publications (she has a 2020 dissertation); any work by Peter Ruščin; anything in *Slovenská hudba*, *Musicologica Slovaca*, or *Cithara* journal

**Category 2: Czech Reformation hymnology — comparative and theological**
- Scholarship on the theological content (not just musicological) of Brethren kancionáls (Šamotulský, Ivančický)
- The Hymnorum Thesaurus Bohemicus (HTB) project — any publications associated with it
- Jan Kouba's *Slovník staročeských hymnografů* (2017) — assess its usefulness for identifying hymn origins
- Anything by Jan Malura on Czech hymnody

**Category 3: Confessional comparison — Lutheran / Utraquist / Unity**
- Zdeněk V. David's work beyond *Finding the Middle Way* — especially articles on Utraquist theology and the Confessio Bohemica
- Craig Atwood's *Theology of the Czech Brethren* (2009) — current scholarly reception, any reviews
- Phillip Haberkern's work on the Confessio Bohemica (2023)
- Any recent comparative study of Eucharistic theology across these three traditions
- Lee Palmer Wandel (ed.), *A Companion to the Eucharist in the Reformation* (Brill, 2014) — check for relevant chapters on Central European Eucharistic practice

**Category 4: Lex orandi, lex credendi methodology applied to Reformation liturgy**
- Any scholarship that uses the *lex orandi* lens to analyse Reformation-era confessional differences through liturgical/hymnological sources
- Bryan Spinks, Christopher Boyd Brown, Robin Leaver — check for recent work on Lutheran hymnody and theology
- David Holeton's publications on Utraquist liturgy (Charles University)

**Category 5: Slovak Reformation and confessionalisation**
- Daniel Škoviera, Karl Schwarz, or other scholars on the Reformation in Upper Hungary / Slovakia
- Anything on the Confessiones Pentapolitana / Heptapolitana / Scepusiana in recent scholarship
- The mining-town Reformation context (Banská Bystrica, Banská Štiavnica) — beyond Benka 2024

### Constraints
- Prioritise peer-reviewed scholarship: monographs from university presses, articles in indexed journals, critical editions.
- I can work in English, Slovak, Czech, German, and (with effort) Latin. Don't exclude non-English sources.
- Flag anything that might directly challenge or complicate my thesis — I want to engage the strongest counter-arguments.
- For each source, note whether it's available digitally or requires library acquisition.

### Output format
Organise findings by category. For each source: full citation, brief relevance note, access information, and a priority tag (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW) for my chapter specifically.
