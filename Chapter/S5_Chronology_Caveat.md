# §5 Framing Argument — The Chronology Caveat

**Status:** Adopted as a §5 framing argument (2026-06-03). The Brethren source datings are empirically grounded; the application to any specific Eucharistic pair is an open question (see below). **Corrected 2026-06-03** after an audit against `GresovaSek_Prispevok` and `taxonomy/hymn_pairs.csv` — the first draft mis-filed the Utraquist Kolínský 1517 as a Brethren source and overstated the Eucharistic application.
**Governs:** Brethren → *Cithara Sanctorum* hymn-transfer pairs in §5. It does **not** govern Utraquist-source pairs (those fall under the David-corpus framing).
**Surfaced from:** `Sources/JHalama_2003.md` (Analytical Note).

---

## The argument

Halama dates the genuine Calvinisation of the Unity of Brethren — its concession of secular-power oversight of doctrine in the Bratrská konfese 1607, its Calvinist-school clergy formation, its turn-of-century Elders' open Calvinism against Turnovský — to roughly **1600–1607** (`JHalama_2003`, pp. 154–155, 190). It is a *late-elite* phenomenon, located in ecclesiology and secular-power doctrine, not in the hymnic corpus.

The **Brethren** hymn material the *Cithara Sanctorum* (1636) absorbs descends from a pre-Calvinisation layer: the Kancionál Jana Roha (`KancRoh_1541`, 1541), the Šamotulský kancionál (1561), and its Ivančický revision (1564), plus Hussite-era survivals — all a generation or more before the drift.

**Therefore:** the confessional boundary Tranovský's *Brethren* transfers cross is **Lutheran ↔ older *Lukán* Brethren**, not Lutheran ↔ late-Calvinist Brethren. The "weren't the late Brethren basically Calvinist anyway?" objection misfires, because the Brethren layer in question predates the drift.

This hardens the boundary in the same direction the Zdeněk V. David corpus hardens it on the Utraquist side: the more distinct the source tradition, the more the "Lutheranisation" register-shift has to account for.

**Scope discipline (audit correction).** Two sources that *look* like they belong here do not. The Utraquist **Kolínský kancionál 1517** is CS's largest single *melodic* source (`GresovaSek_Prispevok`, p. 6) and older than any Brethren layer — but it is **Utraquist**, so material from it falls under the David-corpus framing, not this caveat. And on the *textual* side, CS's most-represented single source is the **Lutheran Závorkov kancionál 1602** (`GresovaSek_Prispevok`, p. 7); the Brethren textual contribution (Roh 1541, third overall) is a real but minority strand. §5 must not inflate it, and must not rest a textual-theological argument on melodic-provenance data.

All three predate Halama's ~1600–1607 window by a generation or more. The caveat's *premise* is solid; its *application to specific pairs* is the weak point (next two sections).

## The enrichment: boundary *relocation*, not just "not Calvinist"

The caveat's payoff is sharper than a negation. The older Lukán Brethren held a *distinctive* Eucharistic grammar — Luke's "modes of being" scheme (1507 four-fold; 1520/1523 essential-vs-ministrative), documented at `Crews_2018`, pp. 22–23 — which is neither Lutheran real presence nor Calvin's position. So *where* a doctrinally-loaded Brethren transfer exists, the "Lutheranisation" is a register-shift across the **Lutheran ↔ Lukán-spiritual-presence** line specifically, tying §5 into the §4.1 Eucharistic comparison.

**But that "where" is itself an open question for the Eucharist.** The one seed Eucharistic Brethren pair — PAIR_002 (*Chléb náš nebeský* → *Pane náš nebeský*, Ivančický 1564 → CS) — is a CONJECTURED methodological *exemplar*, not a documented transfer. Grešová-Sekelská's structural evidence (2021, pp. 8–9) points the other way: Tranovský may have *excluded* Brethren Eucharistic content from CS, much as he excluded the Marian material. So the boundary-relocation may have no Eucharistic hymn to attach to. Resolving it runs through Vajda 1944's attribution table (pp. 74–84), Mladějovská 2008's Ivančický catalogue, and direct CS-vs-Ivančický collation. Until then, §5 states the relocation as a *principle*, not as a reading of a specific pair.

## Vector discipline — three things §5 must not collapse

- **Brethren-chronology vector** (this caveat): Brethren-source transfers cross the Lutheran ↔ Lukán line; relevant only where a Brethren doctrinal pair is confirmed.
- **Utraquist de-Catholicisation vector**: the Marian pair PAIR_001 draws on the **Utraquist** Kolínský 1517; Tranovský's edit removes Catholic resonance (`GresovaSek_Prispevok`, p. 9 — "allusions or hints of Catholicism"). Orthogonal to the Calvinist question; sits under the David-corpus framing.
- **The residual-Calvinist datum**: Grešová-Sekelská (via Mocko, p. 9) notes **two "Calvinist errors" slipped through** Tranovský's Lutheran filter into CS. Minor, but it cuts against any claim the Lutheranisation was total — worth a §5 footnote, and a small reinforcement of §4.1's point that "Calvinist" was a live editorial worry, not a settled label.

---

## Honest caveat — the caveat has no verified pair to stand on yet

The caveat governs Brethren → CS transfers, and the seed dataset has **no verified** one. The only seed Brethren → CS pair, PAIR_002, is `CONJECTURED`/`UNVERIFIED` and may not correspond to a real transfer (above). PAIR_001 is an **Utraquist**-source pair (Kolínský 1517, `SECONDARY_ONLY`, awaiting Baťová 2011), not Brethren. The two VERIFIED pairs (PAIR_004, PAIR_006) sit within the Utraquist/Lutheran orbit. So §5 deploys the caveat as a *framing principle* and states openly that its illustration awaits the PAIR_002 resolution.

---

## Verification hook (one, narrow)

Confirm **which source editions Tranovský actually worked from** (Grešová-Sekelská 2021 / 2023 is the authority on his editorial method), and **attribute each hymn to its datable textual layer, not the print date of the edition** Tranovský physically used — the Závorkov 1602 and later Kralice-press editions were printed *during* the drift but transmit older, conservative texts. The Calvinisation Halama documents touched elite ecclesiology, not hymn texts.

---

## How §5 deploys it

A short framing passage early in §5, before the pair-by-pair case studies: the *Brethren* material CS receives is pre-Calvinisation stock (Roh 1541 / Šamotulský 1561 / Ivančický 1564), so Brethren transfers are read against the older Brethren profile (§3) and the Lukán Eucharistic grammar (§4.1), not against late-Unity Calvinism. Utraquist-source material (Kolínský 1517, de-Catholicisation) is kept separate, under the David-corpus framing. Once PAIR_002 is resolved, any confirmed Brethren Eucharistic transfer is read across the relocated boundary.
