## 1. Úvod (Introduction)

**Purpose:** Situate the chapter within the TEPALIT project; define objectives, methodology, scope.

- TEPALIT context: interdisciplinary project on theological paradigm of liturgical and hymnological sources
- Chapter's specific contribution: dogmatic-comparative dimension
- Methodological framework: four approaches (comparative systematic, historical-critical, lived theology/*lex orandi*, reception history)
- Scope boundaries: 16th–18th century, three confessions, four *loci*
- Explicit statement: we examine "lived theology" through hymns and liturgy, not just normative documents

**Sources:** Project application; methodological literature on comparative theology and *lex orandi, lex credendi*

---

## 2. Konfesionálny kontext (Confessional Context)

**Purpose:** Historical overview of relations between the three traditions in the Bohemian-Hungarian space.

- Hussite roots: from Hus through Four Articles to the Utraquist-Unity split
- Lutheran arrival in Upper Hungary; linguistic proximity enabling transfer
- Phases to distinguish: old-Utraquism vs. neo-Utraquism; early vs. later Unity
- Political context: Habsburg pressures, Confessio Bohemica as convergence attempt
- Post-White Mountain suppression and its long shadow

**Sources:** `Rican_Dejiny`, `David_MiddleWay`, `Fudge`, `Haberkern_2023`; primary: `ArtPrag_1420`, `ConfBohem_1575`

---

## 3. Dogmatické profily (Dogmatic Profiles)

**Purpose:** Characterise each confession's theological identity — their self-understanding, not caricatures.

### 3.1 Luteranizmus augsburského vyznania
- CA, Formula Concordiae as normative backbone
- Regional confessions (Pentapolitana, Heptapolitana, Scepusiana) showing local adaptation
- Key distinctives: *sola fide*, real presence, universal priesthood balanced with ordered ministry

### 3.2 Utrakvizmus (old and neo-)
- Old-Utraquism: conservative liturgical reform within Catholic structures
- Neo-Utraquism: Protestant turn, vernacular liturgy, Agenda Česká 1581
- Ecclesiological middle position: episcopal structure without papal submission

### 3.3 Jednota bratská
- Radical origins (Chelčický), voluntary church model
- Lhotka 1467: independent episcopate via Waldensian succession
- Sacramental evolution: from early radicalism to mature Confessio Fidei (1535)
- Blahoslav generation: hymnological peak (Šamotulský, Ivančický kancionáls)

**Sources:** `BookConcord_1580` (CA, FC), `ConfPentapol_1549`, `ConfHeptapol_1559`, `ConfScepus_1569`, `AgCeska_1581`, `ConfFidei_1535`; secondary: `Atwood_CzBrethren`, `David_MiddleWay`, `Rican_Dejiny`

## 4. Komparatívna analýza (Comparative Analysis) — CORE

**Purpose:** Systematic comparison organised by theological *loci*. Each locus: normative position → liturgical/hymnological manifestation → comparison → identification of convergence/divergence.

### 4.1 Sviatostná náuka — Eucharistia
- Real presence (Lutheran) vs. spiritual presence (mature Unity) vs. conservative Catholic-adjacent (old-Utraquist)
- *Sub utraque specie*: shared practice, different theological grounding
- Sacrificial character of liturgy: how each tradition handled the Mass/Lord's Supper
- **Hymn evidence:** Eucharistic hymns in CS vs. Brethren kancionáls — textual comparison
- **Liturgical evidence:** Agenda Česká 1581 Eucharistic rite (Benka 2024 critical ed., pp. 131–150) vs. Lutheran agendas; Bystrická agenda (1585) as witness to harder Eucharistic formulations — exploit moderate↔hard spectrum for *lex orandi* argument

### 4.2 Ekleziológia
- Church, ministry, apostolic succession
- Unity's Lhotka innovation; Utraquist Venice ordinations; Lutheran *successio doctrinae*
- Confessio Bohemica as convergence attempt — what it papered over
- **Hymn evidence:** ecclesiological themes in spiritual songs

### 4.3 Soteriológia a etika
- *Sola fide* (Lutheran) vs. ethico-practical emphasis (Unity's sanctification and discipline)
- How soteriological accents appear in hymn lyrics — are Brethren hymns "Lutheranised" in this dimension?

### 4.4 Autorita Písma a tradície
- Approaches to biblical authority and patristic sources across confessions
- Role of *bibličtina* (Kralice Bible language) as shared medium

**Sources:** `BookConcord_1580`, `AgCeska_1581`, `Benka_2024`, `KancSamotul_1561`, `KancIvanc_1564`, `KancJisteb_1420`, `CitharaSanct_1636`; secondary: `David_1999`, `Holeton_Lit`, `GresovaSek_2023`

## 5. Transfer a recepcia (Transfer and Reception)

**Purpose:** Case study — how Czech Reformation hymns were received and adapted ("Lutheranised") in Slovak Lutheran use through the Cithara Sanctorum.

- Tranovský's editorial method: selection, adaptation, theological filtering
- Concrete textual comparisons: selected hymns from Brethren kancionáls → CS versions
  - Vocabulary changes (Czech biblical language → Slovakised vernacular)
  - Theological alterations (esp. Eucharistic and soteriological language)
  - Structural modifications (stanza additions/omissions)
- The *bibličtina* medium: linguistic proximity as both enabler and concealer of theological difference
- What the adaptations reveal about boundaries of acceptable theological diversity

**Sources:** `GresovaSek_2023`, `Vajda_1944`, `Mladejovska_2008`, `CitharaSanct_1636`, `KancSamotul_1561`, `KancIvanc_1564`; database: HTB (http://www.clavmon.cz/htb/)

## 6. Záver (Conclusion)

**Purpose:** Synthesise findings; articulate significance for understanding Central European confessionalisation.

- What *lex orandi* reveals that *lex credendi* documents don't
- Surprising convergences and persistent divergences
- Implications for understanding Slovak Lutheran identity formation
- Connection to broader TEPALIT project findings
- Open questions for future research
