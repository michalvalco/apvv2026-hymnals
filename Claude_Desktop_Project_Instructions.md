## Michal's Deliverable

A monograph chapter:

**Dogmatická komparácia luteranizmu a českých konfesií (utrakvizmus, Jednota bratská) v zrkadle liturgických a hymnologických prameňov (16.–18. storočie)**

### Core Thesis (working)
The transfer of hymns from Czech Reformation sources (particularly Unity of Brethren kancionáls) into Slovak Lutheran worship through the Cithara Sanctorum involved systematic theological adaptation — a "Lutheranisation" — that reveals both the depth of dogmatic difference between these confessions and the surprising areas of convergence that linguistic proximity and shared anti-Catholic identity made possible. The *lex orandi, lex credendi* lens exposes tensions that normative confessional documents papered over, particularly in Eucharistic theology and ecclesiology.

### Four Theological Loci
1. **Sacramental doctrine** — Eucharist: real presence vs. symbolism vs. spiritual presence; *sub utraque specie*
2. **Ecclesiology** — Church, ministry, apostolic succession
3. **Soteriology and Ethics** — *sola fide* vs. ethico-practical accents of Czech Reformation
4. **Authority of Scripture and Tradition**

### Methodology (Four-fold)
1. Comparative systematic analysis (normative documents per *locus*)
2. Historical-critical contextualisation (political, social context; developmental phases)
3. Lived theology analysis (*lex orandi, lex credendi* — hymns, prayers, liturgical orders)
4. Reception history (*Rezeptionsgeschichte* — transfer and adaptation through Cithara Sanctorum)

### Proposed Chapter Structure
1. Úvod (Introduction — TEPALIT context, objectives, methodology)
2. Konfesionálny kontext (Historical overview of inter-confessional relations)
3. Dogmatické profily (Characteristics of each confession)
4. Komparatívna analýza (Core — organised by *loci*)
5. Transfer a recepcia (Case study: Cithara Sanctorum adaptation)
6. Záver (Conclusion)
