# Theologos House Style — Reference Guide

**Source:** Valčo, M. – Valčová, K. – Hanus, R. (2022). "Teológia Leonarda Stöckela." *Theologos* XXIV, 2/2022, pp. 125–135.
**Compiled:** 2026-05-08
**Purpose:** Reference for formatting the substantive Reformation-history paper for *Theologos*.
**Journal:** *Theologos: Theological Revue*, Faculty of Greek-Catholic Theology, University of Prešov. ISSN 1335-5570 (print), 2644-5700 (online). Indexed in ERIH PLUS since 2016. Two issues per year (April and October). Editor-in-chief: Mons. prof. ThDr. Peter Šturák, PhD. Michal is Associate Editor.

---

## 1. Document anatomy

The published article runs across these structural elements, in order:

1. **Title** (Slovak, then English) — both centred at top of first page.
2. **Authors** — full names, capitalised, comma-separated. Affiliation immediately below: institution name, faculty.
3. **Abstract** — single English paragraph, no heading, italicised, ~150 words. Located before the body. (Slovak abstract appears in the table of contents; English abstract appears at the head of the article itself.)
4. **Keywords** — English, comma-separated, terminating in a period.
5. **Body** — opens with "Úvod" (or comparable). Section headings are bold, in sentence case (no full caps), no numbering. Subsections rare.
6. **Záver** — closing section, also bold heading.
7. **BIBLIOGRAPHY** — heading in small caps, alphabetised by surname. Located at the end.

No abstract heading (the abstract is signalled by italics or layout).
No "Notes" section — all citations are footnotes.
No appendices.
No author bios at the end of the article (these appear in the journal's "Adresár autorov" / "Addresses of Authors" at the back of the issue).

## 2. Length and proportion

Stöckel article: **9 body pages + 1 page of bibliography ≈ 5,000–6,000 words of body text.**

For the Reformation-history paper, target: **~5,500–6,500 words of body** plus bibliography. This is a Theologos article-length, not a long monograph chapter. Pace and density should match: detailed but compressed, with primary-source quotation foregrounded.

## 3. Footnote citation format

Footnotes are numbered sequentially, page-by-page footer placement. Format follows Slovak academic convention (close cousin of Czech/Hungarian humanities style; not Chicago/Turabian, not MLA, not APA). Key features observable from the Stöckel article:

### 3.1 First citation — full bibliographic data

**Book:**
> STÖCKEL, L.: *Catechesis D. Leonarti Stöckelii Pro ivuentute Bartpheñ[sis] composita* [prekl.: Katechizmus p. Leonarda Stöckela napísaný pre bardejovskú mládež]. Anno 1556. 10. calen. Junij. Rukopis je dostupný v knižnici reformovaného kolégia v Debrecíne: Tiszántúli református egyházkerületi és kollégiumi levéltár, pod signatúrou R 620/7.

Pattern: **SURNAME, Initial.: *Title in italics*. Place: Publisher, Year.** Period after surname-comma-initial; colon between author and title; period after title; place-publisher-year separated by comma; final period.

**Edited volume chapter:**
> BODNÁROVÁ, M.: Reformácia vo východoslovenských kráľovských mestách v 16. storočí. In: KOVAČKA, M. (ed.).: *Bardejovský katechizmus z roku 1581 - Najstaršia slovenská kniha*. Martin: Slovenská národná knižnica, 2013, s. 35 – 52.

Pattern: **SURNAME, Initial.: Title of chapter (no italics). In: EDITOR_SURNAME, Initial. (ed.).: *Volume Title in italics*. Place: Publisher, Year, s. PAGE–PAGE.**
- "In:" introduces the host volume.
- "(ed.)" or "(eds.)" follows the editor's name.
- "s." (Slovak: *strana*) introduces page numbers, followed by a non-breaking space.
- Page range uses an en-dash or hyphen with spaces around it: `s. 35 – 52`.

**Journal article:**
> ŠKOVIERA, D.: Pietas docta. Profil humanistu Leonarda Stöckela. In: PEKAROVIČOVÁ, J., VOJTECH, M. (eds.).: *Studia Academica Slovaca 39*. Bratislava: Univerzita Komenského, 2010, s. 235 – 245.

The Stöckel article does not include a pure journal-article example with volume/issue/year format because most of its sources are book chapters in conference proceedings. For *Theologos* itself, follow this pattern:

> SURNAME, Initial.: Article Title. In: *Journal Title in italics*, ROČNÍK, ČÍSLO, YEAR, s. PAGE–PAGE.

(Confirmed against other Theologos articles in the same issue.)

### 3.2 Subsequent citations — short form

The article re-cites Hajduk multiple times. Footnote 19:
> HAJDUK, A.: *Leonard Stöckel. Život a dielo*. Bratislava : EBF UK, 1995, s. 75.

Footnote 22:
> HAJDUK, A.: *Leonard Stöckel. Život a dielo*. Bratislava: EBF UK, 1995, s. 76.

Footnote 28:
> HAJDUK, A.: *Leonard Stöckel. Život a dielo*. Bratislava: EBF UK, 1995, s. 21 – 22.

**Observation: the article repeats the full citation each time, rather than using "op. cit." or "ibid." abbreviations.** This is conservative even for Slovak academic style — it makes every footnote self-contained for the reader. Recommend the same for the Reformation-history paper: full citation each time, with only the page number changing. Length cost is minor; clarity gain is substantial.

For citations to a manuscript with no pagination per locus (e.g., the *Catechesis Stöckeli*), the article references by section and *quaestio* heading:

> Catechesis Stöckeli, druhá časť, čl. O rozhrešení, otázka: Quid est absolutio?

Pattern: **Title (italics or roman, depending on the document's status), section, sub-section, locus marker (e.g., quaestio).** This is appropriate for liturgical orders, agendas, and other liturgical/catechetical works without modern pagination.

### 3.3 Quotation conventions

**Long quotations (multi-sentence) are integrated into the running text** with double quotation marks (Slovak typographic style: `„...“`). They are not block-indented. Example from p. 130:

> ...kde Stöckel píše: „...ľudia vlastnými silami a skutkami nemôžu Boha uzmieriť..."

For the **English-language Reformation paper**, use British single-quote primary, double-quote nested convention: `'...'` outer, `"..."` inner. Period and comma placement follows British practice (outside the quotation marks unless part of the quoted material).

**Foreign-language quotations** (Latin, Greek, Czech, German) appear in italics, with the Slovak (or English) translation either in the body or in a footnote. The Stöckel article quotes the *Catechesis* Latin titles in roman within footnotes:

> Catechesis Stöckeli, otázka: *Quid est credere in Spiritum Sanctum?*

For the Reformation paper: Czech and Latin quotations should be **in italics, original orthography preserved**, with English translation in parentheses or in a following sentence. Long quotations from primary sources (CB 1575, Agenda Česká, Bystrická agenda, FC SD VII) stay in the original language with an English translation immediately after.

## 4. Bibliography format

The end-of-article BIBLIOGRAPHY heading is in small caps. Entries are alphabetised by surname. Each entry is hung-indented (first line flush left, subsequent lines indented). Capitalisation follows Slovak convention: only proper nouns and the first word of titles are capitalised; subtitles are lower-case after the colon.

Examples from the Stöckel article:

> HAJDUK, A.: *Leonard Stöckel. Život a dielo*. Bratislava : EBF UK, 1995.

> KÓNYA, P.: Kalvinizmus v hornouhorských slobodných kráľovských mestách. In: KÓNYA, P. (ed.).: *Leonard Stöckel a reformácia v strednej Európe. Acta Collegii Evangelici Prešoviensis XI*. Prešov : Prešovská univerzita, 2011.

> SCHWARZ, K.: Präzeptor Hungariae. Über den Melanchthonschüler Leonard Stöckel (1510 – 1560). In: KÓNYA, P. (ed.).: *Prvé augsburské vyznanie viery na Slovensku a Bardejov. Acta Collegii evangelici Prešoviensis, V*. Prešov : PU, 2000.

Differences from footnote format:
- **No page-range** at the end of the bibliography entry, unless it is essential to identify the chapter's location in a reference work.
- **Spacing around colons in city : publisher** is conventionally with spaces (`Bratislava : EBF UK`), but practice varies — the Stöckel article is inconsistent (some entries use `Bratislava: EBF UK`, others `Bratislava : EBF UK`). Pick one and apply consistently. Recommend **no extra space**: `Bratislava: EBF UK, 1995.`

Each entry ends with a period.

For the Reformation-history paper, the funding statement (in English, since the paper is English) should be:

> 1. This work was supported by the Slovak Research and Development Agency under the Contract no. APVV-24-0484.

Or in Slovak (if a bilingual or Slovak-language version is prepared):

> 1. Táto práca bola podporená Agentúrou na podporu výskumu a vývoja na základe Zmluvy č. APVV-24-0484.

Place this on **footnote 1**, attached to the article title (not to the first author's name).

## 7. Headings hierarchy

Observable from the Stöckel article:
- **Section heading** (Úvod, Záver, thematic): bold, sentence case, no numbering. Example: "Obdobie teologického formovania a Stöckelove pôsobenie v Bardejove."
- **Subsection heading**: rarely used. When used, would be set off by italics or by prose convention rather than by a typographic level.
- **No numbered headings** (no "1. Introduction", "2. Background"). Section breaks are signalled purely by the bold heading and the visual blank line.

For the Reformation paper: keep this same convention. The structure I propose for the substantive paper would be:

1. Introduction (no heading — or "Introduction")
2. The codex and its problem (the Bratislava Lyceal Library exemplar)
3. The printed Czech register (Agenda Česká 1581)
4. The manuscript Slovak supplement (Bystrická agenda 1585)
5. Reading the stratification: confessional sharpening within Lutheranism
6. Implications for the Lutheranisation thesis
7. Conclusion

All bold, sentence case, no numbering. Section transitions handled in prose.

## 8. English-language conventions

The Stöckel article is in Slovak. The Reformation paper will be in **British English** (Michal's preference for this venue). Theologos publishes articles in both Slovak and English regularly (the issue's table of contents lists articles in EN, SK, IT, PL).

British English specifics to enforce:
- **-ise/-isation** spellings, not -ize/-ization. (e.g., "Lutheranisation" not "Lutheranization", "criticise" not "criticize", "organisation" not "organization".)
- **Single quotation marks** as primary, double as nested. (Slovak typography uses lower-and-upper `„...“`, but English articles in Theologos follow British/Continental anglophone convention.)
- **Spelling for Latin and Czech terms**: leave as in source language. Italicise on first occurrence; subsequent occurrences may be in roman if the term has become a technical term in the paper's discourse.
- **En-dashes for ranges**: `pp. 78–79`, not `pp. 78-79`.
- **No serial comma** (Oxford comma) by default. Example: "Brethren, Utraquist and Lutheran" not "Brethren, Utraquist, and Lutheran." Some British style guides allow it; if used, apply consistently.
- **Date format**: day month year — `8 May 2026`. Decade as 1570s, not 1570's.
- **Foreign place names**: use the form most familiar to anglophone scholars when one exists (e.g., "Wittenberg" not "Vitemberk"); leave Slovak/Czech place names in their native form when no anglophone form exists (e.g., "Banská Bystrica", "Levoča", "Liptov").

## 9. The Stöckel article's substantive structure (as a model)

For thematic guidance, observe how the Stöckel article organises its argument:

1. **Úvod** (Introduction) — 1 paragraph: introduces the figure, outlines the article's three documents (Catechism 1556, Pentapolitana 1549, Preface to Loci Communes 1561), states the argumentative aim.

2. **Obdobie teologického formovania a Stöckelove pôsobenie v Bardejove** (Stöckel's theological formation and Bardejov ministry) — 2 pages: biographical context essential for reading the documents.

3. **Tri kľúčové dokumenty pre štúdium Stöckelovej teológie** (Three key documents) — 2 pages: introduces each document, locates it historically, sketches its theological character without yet doing close textual work.

4. **Kľúčové teologické dôrazy: sola scriptura, sola gratia, sola fide, solus Christus, extra nos** (Key theological emphases) — 4 pages: this is the analytical core. Close reading of the documents, organised by the four solas + the extra-nos motif.

5. **Záver** (Conclusion) — half a page: synthesises Stöckel's significance. Does not repeat the argument; adds a final synthesising claim ("Stöckel was not a great innovator but a faithful interpreter; his distinctive contribution was the docta pietas formula").

**Proportions:** ~10% introduction, ~20% historical context, ~20% document-by-document orientation, ~40% close analytical work, ~10% conclusion.

For the Reformation-history paper, the proportions can shift toward heavier close-textual work because the paper has only one finding to make (the intra-codex stratification) and needs to defend it primarily through the texts themselves. Suggested:

- ~10% Introduction (state the standard view; promise the complementary finding)
- ~15% The codex and its scholarly framing (Benka 2024 critical edition; the Bratislava exemplar; the question of two-witnesses-bound-together)
- ~25% Agenda Česká 1581: the moderate Czech printed register (verbatim quotes, theological reading)
- ~25% Bystrická agenda 1585: the manuscript Slovak supplement (verbatim quotes, theological reading)
- ~15% Reading the stratification: what the AC→BA shift reveals about confessional identity within the Lutheran orbit
- ~10% Implications for the Lutheranisation thesis + brief mention of the CB 1575 finding (saving the full development for the chapter)
