## Sources confirmed as available locally

### Csepregi, Z. — *Die Confessio Pentapolitana: Fragen nach Autorschaft und Datierung* (2009)

- Full bibliographic citation: CSEPREGI, Zoltán. Die Confessio Pentapolitana: Fragen nach Autorschaft und Datierung. In: KÓNYA, Peter (ed.). *„Nezameniteľné je dedičstvo otcov…“ Štúdie k dejinám a súčasnosti protestantizmu v strednej Európe k osemdesiatym narodeninám biskupa Jána Midriaka*. Acta Collegii Evangelici Prešoviensis X. Prešov: Prešovská univerzita, 2009, s. 73–85.
- Local file: `06 Resources/Dejiny/Domace cirkevne dejiny/Csepregi - Die Confessio Pentapolitana, Fragen nach Autorschaft und Datierung.pdf`
- Key claims usable for §2:
  - The 1548 Pressburg Diet's Article 5 mandated *reformatio catholica* — the restoration of Catholic worship — and Article 11 named anabaptists and "sacramentarians" as heretics to be expelled. The Augsburg Confession was NOT explicitly tolerated; Lutherans had to defend their orthodoxy under pressure (p. 73).
  - The standard story (Stöckel as sole author of the 1549 *Pentapolitana*) is unreliable. There were probably two documents: a 1549 brief confession submitted to the royal commissioners by Prešov's councillors (without naming an author), and a later, more extensive Stöckelian elaboration from c. 1559 that became the canonical text (pp. 74–83).
  - Confirms confessional pressure environment under which Upper-Hungarian Lutheran identity was forged (pp. 73–76).

### Csepregi, Z. — *Náboženské ustanovenia snemu r. 1548* (3-pp. trilingual essay)

- Full bibliographic citation: CSEPREGI, Zoltán. Náboženské ustanovenia snemu r. 1548. (Slovak/Hungarian/German trilingual edition, no editor named; appears to be a stand-alone offprint or web publication, no journal/volume info on the file). 3 pp.
- Local file: `06 Resources/Dejiny/Domace cirkevne dejiny/Csepregi - Nabozenske ustanovenia snemu 1548.pdf`
- **NOTE:** This file lacks publication metadata. Before citing in the published paper, verify the bibliographic source — likely an essay reprinted from a larger volume (perhaps the Acta Collegii Evangelici Prešoviensis series or a similar Prešov publication). Alternatively, cite the longer Csepregi 2009 piece above, which makes the same argument with full apparatus.
- Key claim: the 1548 Diet did NOT tolerate the Augsburg Confession. The protestant historiographical reading is wrong. The Diet aimed at *reformatio catholica*. (p. 1, all three columns concur.)

### Bodnárová, M. — *Reformácia v hornouhorských slobodných kráľovských mestách v 16. storočí* (2018)

- Full bibliographic citation: BODNÁROVÁ, Miloslava. Reformácia v hornouhorských slobodných kráľovských mestách v 16. storočí. In: KÓNYOVÁ, Annamária – KÓNYA, Peter (eds.). *Reformácia v strednej Európe / Reformáció Közép-Európában / Reformation in Mittel-Europa I*. Prešov: Vydavateľstvo Prešovskej univerzity, 2018, s. 132–153 (or similar — I have read pp. 132–143 directly).
- Local file: `06 Resources/Dejiny/Domace cirkevne dejiny/Reformacia v Strednej Europe I, zbornik 2018.pdf`
- Key facts usable for §2:
  - The five royal free cities (Košice, Levoča, Prešov, Bardejov, Sabinov) formed the *Pentapolitana* alliance from the late 15th c.; their cooperation extended to religious affairs (p. 132).
  - Patronage rights (*ius patronatus*) and town-council religious control predated the Reformation and structurally enabled rapid Reformation reception (pp. 133–134).
  - Wittenberg-graduated preachers were the most sought-after; Wolfgang Schustel, Bartholomäus Bogner, and others moved among the Pentapolitana cities and Banská Bystrica (pp. 137, 142, fn. 63).
  - Bardejov requested German preachers from Banská Bystrica in 1540 (fn. 63, p. 142) — *direct documentary evidence of the personnel link between the Pentapolitana cities and the central Slovak mining towns.* This is useful for §2's claim that Wittenberg-Lutheran clergy circulated across the Upper-Hungarian Lutheran network including the mining towns where the AC 1581 was used.

## Working bibliographic format crystallisation (per Theologos style)

Tested formats below correspond to the Theologos 2022 Stöckel article's footnote conventions (full citation each time, no *op. cit.*/*ibid.*):

**First citation pattern (chapter in edited volume):**
> CSEPREGI, Z.: Die Confessio Pentapolitana: Fragen nach Autorschaft und Datierung. In: KÓNYA, P. (ed.).: *„Nezameniteľné je dedičstvo otcov…" Štúdie k dejinám a súčasnosti protestantizmu v strednej Európe k osemdesiatym narodeninám biskupa Jána Midriaka*. Acta Collegii Evangelici Prešoviensis X. Prešov: Prešovská univerzita, 2009, s. 73 – 85, hier s. 75.

**First citation pattern (critical edition / book by single editor):**
> BENKA, P. (ed.): *Agenda Neosoliensia: Agenda Czeská 1581 a Bystrická agenda 1585*. Monumenta Linguae Slovacae X. Bratislava: Univerzita Komenského, 2024, p. 79.

**First citation pattern (multi-volume reference work / journal article):**
> GREŠOVÁ-SEKELSKÁ, A.: Príspevok k vzniku a obsahu Cithary sanctorum. In: *Clavibus unitis*, vol. 10, no. 4 (2021), s. 5 – 14.

In the actual paper draft (English-language), `s.` becomes `p.` / `pp.`, but the structural pattern — surname + colon + initial + colon + title in italics + In: editor + volume in italics + place : publisher + year + page numbers — is preserved across the bilingual usage.
