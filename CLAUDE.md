## Michal's Deliverable

A monograph chapter:

**Dogmatická komparácia luteranizmu a českých konfesií (utrakvizmus, Jednota bratská) v zrkadle liturgických a hymnologických prameňov (16.–18. storočie)**

### Core Argument
The chapter examines how dogmatic differences and convergences between Augsburg Lutheranism,
Utraquism (old and neo-), and the Unity of Brethren manifested in liturgical practice and
hymnography. Primary lens: *lex orandi, lex credendi* — what was sung and prayed reveals
what was believed, often more faithfully than normative confessional documents.

### Four Theological Loci

1. **Sacramental doctrine** — Eucharist: real presence vs. symbolism vs. spiritual presence; *sub utraque specie*; sacrificial character of liturgy
2. **Ecclesiology** — Church, ministry, apostolic succession (crucial for Unity of Brethren and Utraquists)
3. **Soteriology and Ethics** — *sola fide* vs. ethico-practical accents of Czech Reformation (sanctification, discipline in Unity)
4. **Authority of Scripture and Tradition** — Approaches to biblical authority and patristic sources

### Methodology (Four-fold)

1. **Comparative systematic analysis** — Normative documents across confessions per *locus*
2. **Historical-critical contextualisation** — Political, social, cultural context; distinguish developmental phases
3. **Lived theology analysis** (*lex orandi, lex credendi*) — Hymn texts, prayers, liturgical orders (agendas)
4. **Reception history** (*Rezeptionsgeschichte*) — Transfer and adaptation of Czech sources into Slovak Lutheran use (esp. Cithara Sanctorum "Lutheranisation")

### Proposed Chapter Structure

1. Introduction (TEPALIT context, objectives, methodology)
2. Confessional Context (historical overview of relations)
3. Dogmatic Profiles (characteristics of each confession)
4. Comparative Analysis (core: organised by *loci* — Eucharist, Ecclesiology, etc.)
5. Transfer and Reception (case study: Cithara Sanctorum adaptation of Czech hymns)
6. Conclusion

## Working Conventions

### Phase Tracking
This project follows the Research Paper Protocol (five phases). Current phase noted in Project_Overview.md.

### Writing
- Michal's voice: conversational yet scholarly, dramatic sentence-length variation, rhetorical development
- Language: primarily Slovak for the chapter text; English for infrastructure and notes
- Framework: Christian Personalist, monotheistic — operate within this, critique from within
- Forbidden words: "delve," "dive," "navigate," "leverage," "unlock," "crucial," "tapestry"

### Citation Discipline
- Page numbers for all direct quotes — no exceptions
- Prefer primary sources over secondary summaries
- Sources used accurately and fairly; steelman opposing positions
- Key confessional documents cited from critical editions where available

### Key Primary Sources

**Lutheran:**
- Book of Concord (1580), esp. Confessio Augustana, Formula Concordiae
- Regional confessions: Pentapolitana (1549), Heptapolitana (1559), Scepusiana (1569)
- Cithara Sanctorum (Tranovský, 1636+)
- Luther: Formula Missae (1523), Deutsche Messe (1526), De captivitate Babylonica (1520)

**Utraquist:**
- Four Articles of Prague (1420), Basel Compacts (1436)
- Agenda Česká (1581 — scans available in resources)
- Jistebnický kancionál (c. 1420)
- Jan Hus: De ecclesia (1413)

**Unity of Brethren:**
- Confessio Fidei (1535), Acta Unitatis Fratrum
- Šamotulský kancionál (1561), Ivančický kancionál (1564)
- Petr Chelčický: Sieť viery; Lukáš Pražský: sacramental writings
- Jan Blahoslav: Musica (1558/1569)

**Trans-confessional:**
- Confessio Bohemica (1575)
- Biblia Kralická (1579–1593)

### Key Databases
